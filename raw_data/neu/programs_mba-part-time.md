## A flexible MBA fueled by experience
  * Prepare to lead with an innovative MBA program that builds leadership and analytical skills.
  * Collaborate with faculty and experienced entrepreneurs on projects and case studies. 
  * Customize half your MBA with electives and up to two concentrations from 16 market-aligned options.
  * Set your own schedule each semester and apply what you learn through real-world experiences.
  * Study in person on our beautiful, centrally located Boston campus—an ideal place to grow your network and practice collaboration.


## Program options
### Build critical skills to lead with impact in business
Choosing the Part-Time MBA at D'Amore-McKim means choosing a program built for ambitious professionals—flexible, customizable, and designed for real career impact. You'll tailor your experience by selecting up to two concentrations from more than 15 in-demand options, along with electives and learning experiences that connect the classroom to the business world. With access to a vibrant community of peers, faculty mentors, and student organizations, you'll shape your path as a future-ready leader prepared to drive meaningful change.

Complete required coursework and choose electives 
Core courses in accounting, management, marketing, data analysis, and entrepreneurship provide a strong business foundation. Electives make up about half of the MBA, ensuring a broad understanding of modern business challenges and strategies.
#### Optional travel-based courses
Stand out further with experiential electives aligned with your career goals. These may include travel—such as a [week-long residency in Washington, D.C.](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/washington-dc-residency/), to explore how public policy shapes global economies, or international options like the [Global Field Study](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/global-field-study/) or [Global Consulting Project](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/global-consulting-project/), where you'll immerse yourself in another country's business practices.

You'll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Virtual interview (by invitation only)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)
