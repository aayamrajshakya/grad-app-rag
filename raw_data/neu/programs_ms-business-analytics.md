## Program options
### Become a data-driven leader through transformative experiences
Our MS in Business Analytics prepares the next generation of leaders for our data-driven, technology-first world. In this experience-powered, STEM-designated program, you'll learn from world-class faculty as you build essential skills in quantitative analysis techniques, visualization, and information management. When you graduate, you'll have the knowledge and experience you need to tackle real-world business challenges and shape strategic decisions. 
Choose your format, location, and pace of study 
You can pursue the MS in Business Analytics on your terms, choosing the full-time, on-ground option in Boston or Oakland—or a fully online format designed for working professionals. On-campus study immerses you in a collaborative environment with direct access to faculty, peers, and networking opportunities, and international students may qualify for F-1 visa work authorization after graduation. The online option, which typically takes 20 months to complete, delivers the same rigorous, STEM-designated curriculum. Thanks to the flexible pacing, you can balance your studies with career and personal commitments from anywhere in the world.

### Stackable credits
If you're also considering an MBA from D'Amore-McKim, the MS in Business Analytics can get you halfway there. Once your degree is complete—and you've maintained at least a 3.0 GPA and a minimum grade in each class—you may be able to apply your 30 credits toward an eligible MBA program for up to three years following the completion of your degree. Should your application be accepted, you'll be eligible for the [Double Husky scholarship](https://www.northeastern.edu/graduate/double-husky-scholarship/) to reduce your tuition by 25%.

## Applicant FAQs
How do I apply and what will I need? 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our[ application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, answer a few questions, and pay the fee. You'll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)


[Learn more about applying to D'Amore-McKim](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/how-to-apply/)
Are there other application requirements for international students? 
Yes. International students must meet additional requirements, including proof of English language proficiency, a valid passport, and English translations of academic transcripts. Our [international graduate students page](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/faq/international-graduate-students/) provides more information about applying and the benefits of choosing D'Amore-McKim.
Are there opportunities for international students to gain work experience in the U.S.? 
Yes. International students in F-1 immigration status are eligible for a corporate residency using [Curricular Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/f-1-curricular-practical-training/) authorization. When you complete your program, you may become eligible to apply for [Optional Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/applying-for-post-completion-opt-f-1/) (OPT), which, if granted, allows you to work in the United States for up to 12 months. We encourage you to work closely with your career advisor and an advisor in the Office of Global Services.
Is the GMAT or GRE required for admission? 
Northeastern University's D'Amore-McKim School of Business is currently test-optional and does not require applicants to submit GRE or GMAT scores.
Are there any prerequisite courses I need to take before I enroll in the MS in Business Analytics? 
An undergraduate-level, full-semester course in Statistics is required.

What are the differences between an MSBA and an MBA? 
An MS in Business Analytics focuses on data analysis, statistical methods, and technical skills like machine learning and data visualization. It typically takes a year to complete and is ideal for roles in data analysis and business strategy. An MBA provides a broad business education, covering finance, marketing, and leadership. It usually takes 2 years to complete and prepares you for leadership roles across various industries. Both prepare you for business careers, depending on your goals.
