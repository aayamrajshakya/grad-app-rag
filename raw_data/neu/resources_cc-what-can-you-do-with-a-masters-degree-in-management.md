#  What can you do with a master's degree in Management? 
Published: Aug. 9, 2024
Curious about the opportunities a [Master's Degree in Management](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-x/) can offer? This advanced degree can open doors to many exciting career paths. A Master's in Management provides students with the technical skills and general business knowledge to position them to excel in the dynamic challenges of tomorrow. 
This article explores the diverse career options and benefits of earning a Master's in Management. 
## What is an MS in Management?
An MS in Management is a graduate-level business degree that equips students with the necessary skills to advance in their chosen profession. A Master's in Management degree focuses on developing advanced leadership, strategic decision-making, and organizational management abilities, making it ideal for recent graduates, young professionals, and those looking to pivot their careers. 
An MS in Management program is tailored for recent graduates with limited work experience. It offers a flexible curriculum that allows students to set their own pace. Full-time students can complete these programs in as little as 12 months, or working professionals seeking part-time or evening study options can extend their program to 24 months. 
Some programs allow students to customize their learning experience to match their interests. Students gain essential business skills and can specialize in [high-demand concentrations](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-x/boston/). This flexible program lets students tailor their curriculum to their career goals, combining core business foundations with specialized expertise. 
## How can an MS in Management help your career? 
An MS in Management can accelerate careers for recent graduates, career changers, or individuals with limited work experience looking to upskill. 
### Fast-track your career
Completing an MS in Management typically takes less time than pursuing other advanced business degrees, such as an MBA, offering a faster route to a valuable credential. 
### Stack credentials
Credits earned during an MS in Management program are often stackable toward obtaining an MBA or other advanced degrees. This means that individuals can build upon their existing qualifications and seamlessly transition into higher levels of education if they choose to pursue further academic or career goals. Some institutions may offer [alumni scholarships](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/admissions-aid/tuition-financial-aid/scholarships/double-husky-scholarship/) for additional programs once one has completed a degree. 
### Leverage flexibility
One of the key advantages of an MS in Management is its flexibility. Unlike many advanced degrees, including a full-time MBA program, which may require a significant time commitment and full-time attendance, an MS in Management program can often be tailored to align with a current job position or fit within an individual's carer plans. Whether evening classes, part-time study options, or online learning, these programs allow individuals to balance their educational pursuits with their professional responsibilities. 
### Network for career growth
MS in Management programs offer invaluable networking opportunities that can lead to exciting job prospects. Connecting with fellow students, alumni, faculty, and industry professionals enhances career trajectory by providing job referrals, mentorship, and industry insights. Actively engaging in networking events and alumni gatherings expands professional circles, opening doors to opportunities that may not have been accessible. 
### Access career support services
You can access expert career guidance from [graduate career centers](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/graduate-career-center/) to help you align your newly acquired skills with the right role. Whether you require assistance refining your resume, expanding your network, or preparing for interviews, these centers support your career advancement journey.

## Master's in Management vs. MBA: What's the difference? 
While it can be difficult to understand the ​​differences between a Master's in Management and an MBA degree, each program provides a unique set of offerings. 
While an MBA program provides a comprehensive business education focusing on leadership and strategic decision-making, a Master's in Management degree offers a specialized approach with customizable concentrations to align with a student's unique interest. A Master's in Management degree can be completed in less time than an MBA, offering a marketable credential in as little as one year, whereas a full-time MBA typically takes two years. 
Both programs provide valuable skills and opportunities for career advancement, catering to diverse learning styles and professional goals. 
## What jobs require a Master's in Management?
While many jobs don't explicitly require a Master's in Management, having this advanced degree can significantly enhance a professional's qualifications and open doors to various career opportunities. For certain roles, such as management consultants, project managers, or executive positions in corporations, a Master's in Management may be preferred. Additionally, industries such as healthcare, finance, and technology increasingly value candidates with advanced degrees in management due to the complex nature of their operations and the need for strong leadership and strategic decision-making skills. 
## What are the job prospects for candidates with a Master's in Management degree?
In today's evolving business landscape, employers seek versatile managers with a broad range of skills. With a Master's in Management degree, you'll be equipped with the core skills employers seek, including knowledge in accounting, finance, data analysis, and more. Your understanding of these essential skills and your specialized expertise in your chosen area of focus will enable you to launch or advance your career in almost any industry. 
According to the [U.S. Bureau of Labor and Statistics](https://www.bls.gov/ooh/management/home.htm), employment in management occupations is expected to grow faster than the average for all occupations from 2024 to 2034. Approximately 1.1 million openings are projected annually in these fields, driven by employment growth and the need to replace departing workers.
