# Text Analysis
A graph showing data from the Viral Texts project, [ presented](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/nulab/viral-texts-ach2019/) at the Association for Computers and the Humanities 2019 conference by Avery Blankenship.
These resources cover topics that include programming languages like R and Python, introductions to computational methods of text analysis, and natural language processing methods, such as topic modeling and word embedding models.

### Tools and Methods
Concordancing
  * [AntConc](http://www.laurenceanthony.net/software/antconc/) – A concordancing and text analysis toolkit created by Laurence Anthony.
  * [CasualConc](https://sites.google.com/site/casualconc/) – A Mac OSX-native toolkit (AntConc’s Mac version is ported from the PC, and has some bugs).


Exploratory Analysis
  * [Lexos](http://lexos.wheatoncollege.edu/upload) – A tool for scrubbing, chunking, and tokenizing text, in addition to performing modest analysis and visualizing clusters. See: [How to Create Topic Clouds with Lexos](http://scottkleinman.net/blog/2014/07/25/how-to-create-topic-clouds-with-lexos/) – Blog post by Scott Kleinman on using Lexos for topic modeling word clouds.
  * [Voyant Tools](http://voyant-tools.org/) – A simple, yet powerful web-based text analysis and visualization tool.
  * [Word Tree](https://www.jasondavies.com/wordtree/) – A tool that creates [word trees](http://betterevaluation.org/evaluation-options/wordtree) from a block of text.
  * [TEI Publisher](https://teipublisher.com/index.html) – Practice and view demos of text encoding with this online tool.


Topic Modeling
  * [Guided Tour](http://www.scottbot.net/HIAL/?p=19113) – A comprehensive guide to topic modeling by Scott Weingart of Carnegie Mellon University.
  * [Topic Modeling Made Just Simple Enough](http://tedunderwood.com/2012/04/07/topic-modeling-made-just-simple-enough/) – An introduction to topic modeling written by Ted Underwood of the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign.
  * [Topic Modeling Toolbox](http://nlp.stanford.edu/software/tmt/tmt-0.4/) – An alternative to MALLET for LDA topic modeling from Stanford University.
  * [Pulling Out the Stops](https://aclanthology.org/E17-2069) – An article questioning the utility of highly customized or comprehensive stop lists by Alexandra Schofield, Måns Magnusson, and David Mimno.


**[Journal of Digital Humanities’s Special Issue](http://journalofdigitalhumanities.org/2-1/pacing-scholarly-conversations/)** – Special issue of JDH on Topic Modeling in the humanities published in 2012.
  * [Topic Modeling: A Basic Introduction](http://journalofdigitalhumanities.org/2-1/topic-modeling-a-basic-introduction-by-megan-r-brett/) – Introductory article by Megan R. Brett from JDH’s special issue explaining the basic concepts of topic modeling.
  * [Words Alone](http://journalofdigitalhumanities.org/2-1/words-alone-by-benjamin-m-schmidt/) – Article on Latent Dirichlet Allocation’s (LDA’s) limitations by Ben Schmidt.


[MALLET](http://mallet.cs.umass.edu/topics.php) – Website for downloading and installing MALLET, an open-source and Java-based Latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) package.
  * [Topic Modeling Tutorial](http://programminghistorian.org/lessons/topic-modeling-and-mallet) – Tutorial by Shawn Graham, Scott Weingart, and Ian Milligan’s on setting up a command-line environment for using MALLET.


GUI Tools that use MALLET:
  * [Google’s Topic Modeling Tool](https://code.google.com/p/topic-modeling-tool/) – A graphical user interface for doing topic modeling.
  * [Serendip](http://vep.cs.wisc.edu/serendip/) – A system for visualizing topic models by Eric Alexander and Joe Kholmann of the University of Wisconsin-Madison.


Visualizing Text Analysis
  * [Bookworm](https://github.com/Bookworm-project) – A customizable corpus trend visualization tool.
  * [TextPlot](https://github.com/trinker/textplot) – A Python package by David McClure that produces a force-directed network of words in a text, the nodes of which are clustered using estimated kernel densities. See also: [(Mental)Maps of Text](http://dclure.org/essays/mental-maps-of-texts/), a blog post explaining the concept of TextPlot; [TextPlot Refresh – Python 3, PyPi, CLI App](http://dclure.org/tutorials/textplot-refresh/), a blog post on downloading and setting up TextPlot; and [Literary MRIs (or, tuning TextPlot)](http://dclure.org/logs/tuning-textplot/), a blog post on TextPlot’s parameters.


Word Embedding Models
  * [Vector Space Models for the Digital Humanities](http://bookworm.benschmidt.org/posts/2015-10-25-Word-Embeddings.html) – A Blog post by Ben Schmidt which links to his [R package wrapping word2vec](https://github.com/bmschmidt/wordVectors) (word2vec is written in C).
  * [Women Writers Vector Toolkit](http://lab.wwp.northeastern.edu/wwvt/) – Interface for querying terms in word2vec models trained on Women Writers Project corpus.
  * [Word Vectors for the Thoughtful Humanist series](https://wwp.northeastern.edu/blog/category/word-vectors/) – A blog series published by the Women Writers Project on word embedding models.
