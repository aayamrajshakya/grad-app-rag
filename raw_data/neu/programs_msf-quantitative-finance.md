Program overview 
##  Become a financial leader 
Prepare for challenges like fintech implementation and integration, globalization, new regulations, and other industry innovations with a STEM-designated MS in Quantitative Finance. If you want to learn cutting-edge modeling techniques and data analytics combined with financial theory, our leading faculty will prepare you to make an impact from day one with a rigorous curriculum and exposure to up-to-the-moment technical tools.
Your coursework—developed with the input of leading financial services companies—includes not only finance courses but also statistics, analytics, econometrics, and data analytics.
Our STEM designation means graduates may be eligible for a 36-month U.S. work journey via Optional Practical Training (OPT). Accreditation 
Accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
Unique features 
  * STEM-designated programs are eligible for F-1 visa compliant.
  * Qualify to apply for Optional Practical Training (OPT) with an additional OPT STEM Extension.
  * Combine financial acumen with mathematically demanding technical and analytical expertise.
  * Learn from world-class faculty who lead today’s financial services industry.
  * Build experience through an optional graduate co-op at a top company or startup.
  * Apply to join the management team of the 360 Huntington Fund, a live mutual fund where students research, recommend, and choose the stocks.
  * Prepare for the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA®) exam with materials integrated into the curriculum.
  * Get personalized career support at the Graduate Career Center.


Career outlook 
The D’Amore-McKim MS in Quantitative Finance program equips you to stand out in the financial services industry with significant technical expertise. Depending on your work experience, your strong quantitative skills can position you to pursue finance roles such as asset manager, data analyst, financial consultant, or investment trader—positions that are sought-after, well-paid, and offer opportunities for career advancement. Companies of all sizes heavily recruit our graduates, from startups to multinational organizations.
Gain real-world experience 
Experience is the signature feature of a Northeastern education. Your MS in Quantitative Finance courses will include case studies and applied project work that strengthen your collaboration and problem-solving skills. You’ll enhance your financial knowledge through unique experience-based activities, including travel opportunities:

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)


Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
The financial services industry needs skilled people with data analytics and econometrics training now more than ever. Learn cutting-edge modeling techniques, data analytics, and financial theory to make an impact from day one.
Advanced techniques 
Learn to apply advanced mathematical and statistical techniques to analyze financial markets and manage risk.
Proficiency with financial analysis tools 
Your coursework draws from coding, engineering, mathematics, statistics, and econometrics—giving you experience with tools for intelligent financial analysis, such as Python and advanced Excel.
Apply your skills on a co-op 
Apply what you learn to a significant project at a leading company or startup. During your co-op, you’ll get a feel for the company’s work culture and develop your teamwork skills while building your professional resumé.
[CFA-aligned curriculum](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-finance/)
Study a curriculum designed to prepare you for the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA®) exam.
