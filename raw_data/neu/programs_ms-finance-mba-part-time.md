## Become a finance leader ready to make an impact 
  * With nine shared credits, earn two degrees faster and gain the leadership skills and deep foundational knowledge in finance that companies want.

## Applicant FAQs
When would I start and when would I graduate? 
Classes begin in September. As a Part-Time MS in Finance/MBA student, you'll typically finish the program in less than four years. Degrees are conferred in May, August, and December with a graduation ceremony held at the end of the spring term.
How do I apply and what will I need?

Is the GMAT or GRE required for admission? 
Northeastern University's D'Amore-McKim School of Business is currently test-optional and does not require applicants to submit GRE or GMAT scores. 
Are there other application requirements for international students? 
Yes, international students must meet additional requirements, including proof of English language proficiency, a valid passport, and English translations of academic transcripts. Our [international graduate students page](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/faq/international-graduate-students/) provides more information about applying and the benefits of choosing D'Amore-McKim. 
Is a master's degree in Finance worth it? 
Employers worldwide seek experts capable of managing portfolios, minimizing risks, maximizing returns, and fostering global growth. However, it's important to consider the time and financial commitment required to gain an advanced finance degree.
[learn more about the degree and its benefits](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/resources/cc-is-an-ms-in-finance-worth-it/)
Will the program prepare me for the CFA? 
The curriculum is closely aligned with the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation. This alignment provides a strong foundation for those who pursue the designation. Learn more at the [CFA Institute website](https://www.cfainstitute.org/en/). 
What kinds of jobs do MS in Finance graduates get? 
A number of possible careers await our graduates, from financial analyst to senior banker. Other titles may include assistant trader, business operations analyst, portfolio manager, investment associate, or risk and compliance manager. After gaining more work experience, the following positions are attainable: chief financial officer, commercial lending officer, consultant, corporate finance officer, director of finance, chief investment officer, or even vice president of mergers and acquisitions.
Is the program accredited? 
Yes. The D'Amore-McKim School of Business is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
