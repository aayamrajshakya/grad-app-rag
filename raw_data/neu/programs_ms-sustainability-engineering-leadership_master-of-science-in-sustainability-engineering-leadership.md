Program overview 
##  Meet the demand for skilled leadership 
The Master of Science in Sustainability Engineering Leadership is a double-degree program from Northeastern University and University College Dublin. The degree focuses on engineering design, development, and assessment of sustainable technologies, coupled with the engineering management and leadership skills needed to bring these technologies to market.
Students select one of the following concentrations:
  * Energy and Climate Technologies
  * Sustainability Assessment


Additionally, students pursue an industry-based research thesis or leadership challenge.
The first year of the program is in Boston and the second year is spent in Dublin. This program meets F1 compliance for the Northeastern first year.

Unique features 
  * Engage in a double-degree program offered in two countries (U.S. and Ireland) at top engineering schools, providing wider career options and deepened knowledge.
  * Partner with the established and sought-after Gordon Institute for Engineering Leadership at Northeastern.
  * Gain the option to participate in Northeastern’s top-ranked cooperative education program and graduate with real-world experience.
  * Build a foundation for pursuing doctoral-level research if desired.

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Graduates of the program are prepared for in-demand senior technical sustainability positions in engineering and industrial firms, consultancies, and environmental organizations.
Drive sustainability improvements 
Develop the skills needed to quantify the sustainability of a product, process, or system; identify areas for improvement; and devise and manage measures for implementation to improve sustainability.
Optimize engineering decisions 
Apply engineering management and decision-making tools to evaluate the technical and economic potential of sustainable technologies.
Lead sustainability initiatives 
Build the capacity to lead the implementation of sustainability principles within an organization through an understanding of technical requirements, quantitative skills, critical analysis skills, and leadership skills.
