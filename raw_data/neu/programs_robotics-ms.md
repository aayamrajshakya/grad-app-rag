# MS in Robotics
[Apply now](https://www.khoury.northeastern.edu/apply/masters-apply/)
[Request info](https://www.khoury.northeastern.edu/programs/robotics-ms/#have-questions)
In the MS in Robotics — an interdisciplinary program between Khoury College and the [College of Engineering](https://coe.northeastern.edu/) — you’ll investigate the science and engineering of robotics. Through rigorous courses and hands-on experiences, you’ll gain a comprehensive understanding of the algorithms, sensors, control systems, and mechanisms of robotics as you prepare to make a transformational impact on society.
[Explore program requirements in the course catalog](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/graduate/engineering/electrical-computer/robotics-ms/#programrequirementstext)

## Take your career to the next level
Career prospects: A wealth of opportunities 
##  Career prospects: A wealth of opportunities 
This program will prepare you for diverse and rewarding career opportunities in the rapidly evolving field of robotics. Some careers of recent graduates:
  * Software support systems at Amazon Robotics
  * Autonomy and robotics software engineer for Aerospace Applications at MORSE Corporation
  * Robotics and control software engineer at Optimus Ride
  * Software integrator at Amazon Robotics


Co-ops and internships: Rated #1 
##  Co-ops and internships: Rated #1 
Through our renowned co-op program, ranked #1 in the country by U.S. News & World Report, you’ll have opportunities to apply your skills and knowledge in industry settings while you build invaluable connections. Recent positions held by co-op students include:
  * Robotics software engineering intern
  * Advanced robotics co-op
  * Machine learning co-op: natural language processing
  * UAV/robotics engineering co-op
  * Mobile robotics software engineer


[Learn more about co-ops and internships](https://www.khoury.northeastern.edu/graduate-co-ops/)

##  Research opportunities: Pursue your passion

Whether you’re interested in a research career, applying to PhD programs, or simply building your expertise through hands-on experience, our diverse range of research projects will give you opportunities to make meaningful contributions.

Rob Platt is an associate professor at Khoury College, affiliated with the College of Engineering.
