The Doctor of Professional Studies (DPS) is an advanced, practice-based research degree designed for midcareer professionals seeking to enhance their professional expertise and leadership capabilities. The program invites professionals from a wide range of disciplines—empowering you to research, develop, and communicate solutions to complex problems and become a co-creator of knowledge and innovation in your workplace.
The program fosters significant personal and professional growth. As a student, you will develop advanced skills and knowledge, leading to increased career opportunities. The curriculum’s focus on critical thinking and leadership equips you to excel in your chosen field.
Online coursework provides a flexible educational journey that allows you to tailor your learning path.
### Program Concentrations
Project management  
This concentration serves as a terminal credential in advanced project management. You’ll have opportunities to engage in a range of advanced topics, including strategic project leadership, emerging technologies, systems thinking, stakeholder management, and program and portfolio management.
Organizational leadership studies  
Equip yourself with advanced knowledge and skills in leadership, behavior, global awareness, and technology within an organizational environment. You’ll study leadership in modern organizations, helping you excel in leadership roles that demand strategic vision, cultural intelligence, and technological advancements. You’ll explore cutting-edge theories and practical applications essential for organizational success in an evolving global landscape.
### Program Benefits
  * Gain practical knowledge and skills that increase your career opportunities.
  * Accelerate your personal and professional growth with a focus on critical thinking and leadership.
  * Learn to drive meaningful change and innovation in your field through research-based learning.
  * Build your understanding of theoretical concepts and how to apply them effectively.
  * Develop and execute a practice-based dissertation that showcases your ability to address tangible issues in your field.

###  Application Requirements 
  * [Online Application](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/)
  * Statement of Purpose: Respond to both questions separately and stay within the prescribed word limits. The ability to demonstrate clear, succinct, well-reasoned writing is essential 
    * Discuss your career trajectory and your professional goals. How will earning this degree help you achieve those goals? (500 words)
    * Identify an important problem or issue related to your profession. Why do you want to explore and understand this problem or issue? Be sure to articulate how this problem or issue relates to your professional field. (500 words)
  * Professional Resumé: Current resumé that displays job responsibilities, relevant experience, and education history.
  * Academic Transcripts: Unofficial undergraduate and graduate transcripts; official transcripts required at the time of admission. 
    * The Foreign Credential Evaluation (FCE) is a required assessment of all transcripts and documents from non-U.S. accredited post-secondary education institutions. (Review the [FCE requirements](https://northeastern.my.site.com/GraduateAdmissionsFAQ/s/article/What-documents-are-required-for-a-credential-evaluation) by country.)
  * Three Letters of Recommendation: 
    * One Academic Recommendation: This letter should come from a former professor or mentor who can speak to your ability to succeed in an academic environment. The recommender should address your capacity to conduct research and write at the doctoral level. If you have been out of school for a long time or are no longer acquainted with your professors, you may ask someone who has evaluated you during a professional training seminar or workshop to compose this recommendation.
    * Two Professional Recommendations: One must be from your current supervisor; recommendations should not come from individuals who report to you. Recommenders are encouraged to identify specific examples that highlight your skills and abilities.
  * Interview: Applicants may be requested to participate in an interview as part of the admissions process. If this is determined, we will reach out to you directly.
  * DPS Transfer Credit: Transfer credits from other institutions are not accepted. Prior Learning Assessment will be considered, and credit/advanced standing will be awarded as appropriate.
