Program overview 
##  Keep clinical research compliant 
Clinical research regulations change quickly and constantly. In the Graduate Certificate in Clinical Research Regulatory Affairs program, you’ll learn to keep up with new regulations and maintain patient safety as you help organizations develop groundbreaking technologies.
As a graduate of this program, you’ll be able to understand global standards and requirements, research processes, key compliance techniques, and the differences in clinical and non-clinical regulations. With alumni working at major companies worldwide, you’ll also be connected to a valuable network of peers who can help you advance your career.

Unique features 
  * Gain a global perspective as you study alongside students from more than 20 countries.
  * Connect with alumni working at the world’s largest pharmaceutical, biologic, medical device, nutraceutical, and cosmeceutical product and service manufacturers including Genzyme, Boston Scientific, Edwards Lifesciences, PRA Health Sciences, Alexion Pharmaceuticals, Pfizer, Novartis, Merck, Nestlé, PepsiCo, and Coca-Cola.
  * Stack credits from this certificate program into Northeastern’s [Master of Science in Regulatory Affairs](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-regulatory-affairs-cps/master-of-science-in-regulatory-affairs-boston/).

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Build your foundation in regulatory affairs through classes and projects based on real-world challenges.
Master global regulatory standards 
Develop a comprehensive understanding of evolving international biomedical product regulatory standards and compliance requirements.
Navigate clinical research processes 
Gain expertise in the complex regulatory processes specific to clinical research and patient-facing biomedical product development.
Ensure regulatory compliance 
Build professional competencies required to conduct regulatory processes for new biomedical products in accordance with global standards and regulatory agency requirements.
Differentiate between regulation types 
Understand the separation between clinical regulatory processes and those applicable to indirect, non-patient-facing development.
