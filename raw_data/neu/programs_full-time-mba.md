Complete required coursework and choose electives 
Complete core courses in accounting, finance, management, marketing, data analysis, and entrepreneurship in your first term—building a strong foundation and creating room to tailor your education early. Electives make up about half the MBA, giving you the flexibility to explore modern business challenges and interests beyond business. With six required interdisciplinary (non-business) credits and access to courses across Northeastern's six graduate colleges, you'll work with your advisor to design a truly customized path aligned with your goals.
#### Optional travel-based courses

These may include travel—such as a [week-long residency in Washington, D.C.](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/washington-dc-residency/), to explore how public policy shapes global economies, or international options like the [Global Field Study](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/global-field-study/) or [Global Consulting Project](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/global-consulting-project/), where you'll immerse yourself in another country's business practices.

Are there other application requirements for international students? 
Yes. International students must meet additional requirements, including proof of English language proficiency, a valid passport, and English translations of academic transcripts. Our [international graduate students page](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/faq/international-graduate-students/) provides more information about applying and the benefits of choosing D'Amore-McKim. 
Are there opportunities for international students to gain work experience in the United States? 
Yes. International students in F-1 immigration status are eligible for a corporate residency using [Curricular Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/f-1-curricular-practical-training/) authorization. When you complete your program, you may become eligible to apply for [Optional Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/applying-for-post-completion-opt-f-1/) (OPT), which, if granted, allows you to work in the United States for up to 12 months. We encourage you to work closely with your career advisor and an advisor in the Office of Global Services.
Is the GMAT or GRE required for admission? 
Northeastern University's D'Amore-McKim School of Business is currently test-optional and does not require applicants to submit GRE or GMAT scores. 
How long is an MBA program? 
Most students complete our Full-Time MBA program in 2 years. The length of your program often depends on the corporate residency you choose, which can last 3, 6, or up to 12 months.
[Learn more about program length](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/news/how-long-is-an-mba-program/)
What is an MBA internship? 
At D'Amore-McKim, MBA students gain real-world experience through corporate residency—a deeper, longer internship where you work alongside company leaders for 3, 6 or up to 12 months. It's a chance to apply what you've learned, build your network, and make a real impact.
[Learn more about MBA Internships](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/resources/cc-what-is-an-mba-internship/)
