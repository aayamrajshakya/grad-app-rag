Program overview 
##  Energize your career 
You will learn about current and emerging energy technologies, as well as the economic and financial aspects necessary for their implementation. You will also develop leadership and decision-making skills to implement energy systems in either the private or public sectors of the global market. Additionally, you will be exposed to a combination of academic and corporate experience.
The curriculum has four core courses in engineering and finance in addition to four electives that relate concepts such as power strategies, energy renewal, sustainable energy solutions, energy storage, energy conversion, and energy efficiency.
The program is ideal for the engineer or technical business major interested in pursuing an industrial or public planning career.

Admissions requirements 
  * Completed online application form
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Transcripts from all institutions attended 
    * The Foreign Credential Evaluation (FCE) is a required assessment of all transcripts and documents from non-U.S. accredited post-secondary education institutions. (Review the [FCE requirements](https://northeastern.my.site.com/GraduateAdmissionsFAQ/s/article/What-documents-are-required-for-a-credential-evaluation) by country.)
  * GRE is not required for terms starting during the 2025-2026 academic years
  * Statement of purpose
  * Resumé
  * TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo for international applicants unless the waiver requirements are met
  * An undergraduate degree in engineering is preferred. Quantitative business or finance majors are acceptable if candidates also have some background in computer science and calculus.
  * The application fee is waived for all new applicants applying to any of our Master of Science or Graduate Certificates for the fall 2025 and spring 2026 application terms.


Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Prepare to effectively integrate energy system development over a broad spectrum of technologies with the financial requirements to successfully implement them and to compete in the global energy market.
Master in-demand skills 
Gain the skills for involvement in decision making and policy planning to deliver minimally polluting, energy-efficient systems to the global market.
Drive energy innovation and efficiency 
Lead efforts to plan and implement energy generation investments, improve system-level energy efficiency, and participate in energy and environmental markets like cap-and-trade systems.
