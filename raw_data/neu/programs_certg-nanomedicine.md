Program overview 
##  Begin your nanomedicine career 
Northeastern University is proud to host one of the world’s first certificates in nanomedicine. This program is ideally suited for students seeking to learn more about nanomedicine before entering the workforce, as well as working professionals who want to gain new knowledge and skills.
We bring together scientists and engineers to study alongside prospective clinicians and healthcare professionals in order to improve human health. This experiential learning program is ideal for individuals seeking careers in biotechnology, pharmaceutical, biomedical, or clinical fields.
As a nanomedicine certificate student, you will earn more than just an education—you will be prepared to innovate, collaborate, and lead industry projects in nanomedicine.

Program details 
  * The certificate program is based in Boston, MA, a city known as a hub for world-class biomedical research, life science innovation, pharmaceutical corporations, and biotech companies.
  * Courses can be taken in person or online (in both synchronous or asynchronous formats), allowing for maximum flexibility.
  * The certificate consists of five courses for a total of 12 semester-hour credits.
  * All classes are in the late afternoon or evening, making the program accessible to working professionals.
  * No prior knowledge of nanomedicine is required.


Experiential learning 
Our signature experience-powered learning model combines world-class academics with professional practice, allowing you to put your ideas into action.
As a nanomedicine student, you can choose to learn and blog about enabling innovations in nanomedicine, conduct experiments in a live online laboratory, create a virtual startup company under industry mentors, and more.
The certificate program offers a dynamic, transformative experience that can be tailored to your interests and needs, giving you opportunities to grow as a professional and a person.

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Students receive training in the rapidly growing field that uses nanotechnology to solve problems in drug delivery and medicine.
Develop expertise 
Program participants study both fundamental and applied aspects of nanomedicine, and then apply this knowledge to designing strategies for nanomedicine innovation, translation, and commercialization.
Professional development 
Students engage in activities for professional development, including lectures, case studies, journal readings, term projects, and close interactions with experts drawn from academia, hospitals, and government.
