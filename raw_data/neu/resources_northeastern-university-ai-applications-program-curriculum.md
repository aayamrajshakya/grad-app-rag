# Northeastern’s AI Applications Program Curriculum: What You Need to Know
Mar 18, 2025[Artificial Intelligence (AI)](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/categories/ai/), [Business & Management](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/categories/business-management/), [Health Sciences](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/categories/healthcare/)
Artificial intelligence (AI) is transforming industries at an unprecedented pace, leaving professionals across fields eager to understand its applications and potential. Whether you're in healthcare, business, education, or another sector, the ability to harness AI tools and insights can position you as a leader in your field. But with so many AI programs available, finding one that aligns with your industry, background, and expertise can feel overwhelming.[Northeastern’s Online Graduate Certificate in AI Applications](https://online.northeastern.edu/certificates/ai-applications/) stands out by offering a practical, interdisciplinary approach designed for professionals at all levels. This program doesn’t just teach AI concepts—it ensures learners can apply them effectively in real-world scenarios, no matter their industry or technical expertise.In this article, we’ll explore what makes Northeastern’s program unique and introduce you to some of the courses you’ll encounter.
## What Makes Northeastern’s AI Applications Program Unique?
### Designed for Non-Technical Professionals
Professionals from all industries can benefit from Northeastern’s AI Applications certificate, which is uniquely designed to be [accessible even to those without a technical background](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/ai-courses-for-non-technical-professionals/). The program focuses on providing a strong foundation in AI and empowering participants to apply its capabilities effectively within their respective fields.The curriculum is intentionally structured to bridge the gap between technical concepts and practical applications. Instead of requiring advanced coding or data science skills, it emphasizes real-world use cases so learners can understand AI principles and confidently apply them to solve diverse business challenges. For instance, you’ll explore how machine learning can enhance customer personalization or streamline supply chain operations without delving into algorithm design. These examples show how AI can be applied in real-world scenarios to help you gain practical skills to drive impact in your industry.
### A Multidisciplinary Approach
Northeastern is known to leverage its extensive network of schools to bring together faculty expertise from a wide range of disciplines to create uniquely collaborative programs. This multidisciplinary approach is a hallmark of the AI certificate, providing learners with diverse perspectives and insights from multiple fields. “You are able to get multidisciplinary perspectives on the same set of technologies,” says Vance Ricks, an associate teaching professor in [Northeastern’s Online Graduate Certificate in AI Applications program](https://online.northeastern.edu/certificates/ai-applications/). “Which can really help to broaden your understanding of AI’s impact across industries​.”This approach is particularly valuable because it mirrors the complex, interconnected nature of AI applications in the real world. For example, a healthcare professional might learn how AI-powered predictive analytics can improve patient outcomes while also gaining insights from business strategies to optimize operational efficiency. By connecting ideas across disciplines, professionals are better equipped to adapt innovative solutions from one field to another and leverage creative problem-solving effectively.
### Capstone Projects for Real-World Application
Capstone projects are a key feature of Northeastern’s AI Applications Program, giving students the chance to solve real-world problems. These projects focus on challenges faced by companies, allowing participants to apply their AI skills in meaningful and practical ways. This hands-on approach ensures graduates are prepared to make an immediate impact in their fields.
> “Most courses that include project-based learning are designed to ensure every student, regardless of background, can contribute to a meaningful project while gaining practical, real-world experience,” Vance adds.​
For example, students can work on projects that develop AI models to optimize patient scheduling in a hospital setting. Or, learners can explore how AI could enhance learning outcomes by analyzing patterns in student performance data to tailor educational resources more effectively.
## Northeastern’s AI Applications Curriculum
The program offers a range of courses designed to build foundational knowledge and practical skills in AI. Each course focuses on the tools and insights needed in [today’s automated industries](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/ai-and-jobs/), regardless of your technical background.
### Exploring AI Trends and Tools
A clear understanding of AI's foundational concepts and practical applications is critical if you’re hoping to adapt to rapid technological advancements in your industry. This knowledge not only helps you navigate the evolving AI landscape but also prepares you to address its societal and ethical implications more effectively.To meet this need, Exploring AI Trends and Tools, taught by Professor Prashant Mittal from the Roux Institute, provides a comprehensive and accessible introduction to AI. The course covers core topics such as AI-enabled perception, reasoning, and learning, with a strong focus on societal impact and ethical considerations. Through hands-on projects and a summative case study, you’ll develop the skills to collaborate across disciplines, simplify complex AI concepts, and apply AI tools to solve real-world challenges.
### Human-Centered AI
As AI becomes increasingly integrated into our daily lives, designing systems that prioritize human values and address ethical challenges is essential. The course Human-Centered AI, taught by Steven Geofrey, Associate Teaching Professor at Northeastern’s College of Arts, Media, and Design, explores how AI can be developed to enhance human potential rather than replace it.Through topics such as transparency, accountability, and trust, the curriculum examines critical questions like how to ensure AI aligns with human goals and mitigates risks such as bias and harm. You’ll be able to engage with real-world case studies to evaluate how AI impacts decision-making processes to uncover the trade-offs between technical capabilities and societal outcomes.“We’re not here to let AI happen to us,” says [Steven Geofrey](https://camd.northeastern.edu/people/steven-geofrey/), an associate teaching professor in [Northeastern’s Online Graduate Certificate in AI Applications program](https://online.northeastern.edu/certificates/ai-applications/). “We’re here to ask the hard question of, how do we design the relationship between humans and AI?”
### Responsible AI
As AI continues to transform industries and society, it raises urgent questions about fairness, accountability, and how to ensure these technologies reflect and uphold societal values. The course Responsible AI, taught by Dr. Vance Ricks, Associate Teaching Professor in Northeastern’s College of Social Sciences & Humanities, gives you the tools to address these challenges and navigate the ethical complexities of AI.The curriculum covers foundational topics such as governance, compliance, and regulatory considerations in relation to the trade-offs inherent in [AI-based decision-making](https://online.northeastern.edu/resources/ai-in-business/). It also examines critical issues like privacy, transparency, bias, and fairness through real-world case studies. 
> “It’s crucial to ask not just whether we can use AI tools, but whether we should, and in what ways they align with our values and goals,” Vance says. 
### Strategic AI for Business
Integrating AI into strategic decision-making is essential for businesses looking to innovate and solve complex challenges. The course Strategic AI for Business, taught by Kwong Chan, Senior Academic Specialist in Marketing and Executive Director of the DMSB AI Strategic Hub, equips you with the tools and knowledge to apply AI effectively in business contexts.The curriculum focuses on practical applications of AI, including supervised, unsupervised, and reinforcement learning, to generate data-driven insights and address business problems. Using frameworks like SWOT and the resource-based view, students learn to develop strategies that align AI capabilities with organizational objectives. The course also emphasizes ethical considerations and the intersection of AI technologies with managerial practices.
