Program overview 
##  The future of cities starts here 
With 75% of the world’s population projected to be living in cities by 2050, the need for professionals in urban planning and related careers is increasing. The Graduate Certificate in Urban Analytics prepares students to manage the complex issues involved with rapidly expanding data and technological resources in cities.
Students will be trained in the intricacies of interconnected urban systems with the practical knowledge to analyze how these systems work together. Focusing on Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and related technology, the program gives you the skills necessary to shape the future of cities across the globe.
The curriculum emphasizes the expertise needed to bridge emerging technological capacities and traditional policy-making processes to help create sustainable, resilient, and just cities.

Unique features 
  * The curriculum provides both practical and theoretical knowledge
  * Students will cultivate applied skills in visual presentation, analysis, and modeling of new datasets—all of which will help to inform investment and policy making
  * Students use Boston and cities around the world as learning labs
  * Students may choose to complete a research practicum
  * The certificate may be completed as a stand-alone graduate program or in conjunction with a graduate degree program at Northeastern
  * This certificate can stack into the Master of Science in Urban Informatics program

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
A graduate certificate in Urban Analytics prepares you for success in this pioneering field at the intersection of cities and data.
Gain applied skills in urban analytics 
Students cultivate applied skills in visual presentation, analysis, and modeling of new data sets—all of which helps to inform investment and policy making.
[Groundbreaking research](https://www.northeastern.edu/cssh/policyschool/urban-informatics/research-projects/)
Students collaborate with faculty on use-inspired research in class and in our research centers-[The Kitty and Michael Dukakis Center for Urban and Regional Policy](http://www.northeastern.edu/dukakiscenter/), [The Social Impact Lab](http://www.northeastern.edu/impactlab/), and the [Boston Area Research Initiative](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/bari/).
The city is your classroom 
Inspired by Northeastern’s leadership in experiential education, students will use Boston and cities around the world as learning labs.
Lead in an emerging field 
Only a handful of U.S. universities currently offer certificates in urban analytics.

Degree building 
##  Stack your certificate 
Earning a graduate certificate is a fast and affordable way to acquire specialized skills while building toward your master’s degree. Upon completion of this certificate program, you may be able to apply your credits to an eligible master’s program, saving time and money on your degree.
Some of the eligible master’s programs for this certificate include:
  * [Master of Science in Criminology and Criminal Justice](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-criminology-crim-justice/master-of-science-in-criminology-and-criminal-justice/)
  * [Master of Science in Applied Quantitative Methods and Social Analysis – Boston](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-app-qnt-methods-soc-anlys/master-of-science-in-applied-quantitative-methods-and-social-analysis-boston/)
  * [Master of Public Administration](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/mpa-public-administration/master-of-public-administration-boston/)
  * [Master of Public Policy](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/mpp-public-policy/master-of-public-policy/)
  * [Master of Science in Urban Informatics](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-urban-informatics/master-of-science-in-urban-informatics-boston/)
