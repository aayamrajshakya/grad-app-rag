Programs 
##  Find your graduate program 
If you’re ready to level up your career and become a leader in your field, Northeastern is where you want to be. Here, you can choose from 200-plus tailored master’s, professional doctorate, PhD, and certificate programs, and join a global community designed to help you succeed and achieve your professional goals.
Master’s degrees 
Learn new skills, deepen your knowledge, and gain valuable experience with a master’s degree. Our programs combine expert-led classroom study with real-world opportunities to help you advance your career and create meaningful impact.
  * [ See master’s degrees](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/?degree_types=masters)


Professional doctorates 
Combine academic coursework with applied research projects to gain deep, industry-specific knowledge and develop solutions to real-world problems facing your workplace or community. Gain industry insights from faculty researchers in law and policy, education, nursing, and more.
  * [ See professional doctorates](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/?degree_types=professional-doctorate)


PhDs 
Conduct original research alongside world-class faculty to solve some of the world’s most pressing problems. These programs offer dedicated mentoring, robust resources, and experiential opportunities inside industry, government, nonprofits, and academia.
  * [ See PhD programs](https://phd.northeastern.edu/)


Graduate certificates 
Graduate certificates are a fast and affordable way to acquire specialized skills to advance your career or build toward a master’s degree. Choose from more than 120 programs that feature four to six focused courses and can be completed in as little as six months.
  * [ See graduate certificates](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/?degree_types=certificate)

###  Ammar Aamer 
College of Professional Studies 
Ammar Aamer is an associate teaching professor who brings more than 23 years of industry experience consulting for companies like Volvo, TRW Automotive, and Gap Inc. His research focuses on data analytics and supply chain management, artificial intelligence and project management, Lean Six Sigma, entrepreneurship, and the Internet of Things (IoT). 
###  Sami Rollins 
Khoury College of Computer Sciences 
Sami Rollins is a teaching professor and the director of graduate computing programs for the Oakland campus. She contributes to S-STEM projects funded by the National Science Foundation and has served in a number of service roles. Her research interests include broadening participation in computing, computer science education, and mobile and distributed computing. 
###  Tommaso Melodia 
College of Engineering 
Tommaso Melodia is the William Lincoln Smith chair professor in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, the founding director of the Institute for the Wireless Internet of Things, and a National Science Foundation CAREER Award winner. His current NSF-funded research includes the modeling, optimization, and experimental evaluation of Internet-of-Things and wireless networked systems.
