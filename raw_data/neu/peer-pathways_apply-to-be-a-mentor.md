# Apply to be a Mentor!
Are you a master’s student on the Boston campus who has completed at least one semester? Are you interested in connecting with a new student who’s learning the ins and outs of how to be a successful graduate student at Northeastern University? Would you like to receive formal mentoring training and become part of an exciting community with workshops, events, and swag? Do you like to meet new people and love to help others? If so, we invite you to become a mentor by applying below! 
**Please be sure to read all requirements before applying!
# Important Info - READ BEFORE APPLYING
Z
#### Interdisciplinary
The Peer Pathways Program is interdisciplinary in nature, and there’s a chance that you will connect with someone who is not part of your academic program at Northeastern. Efforts will be made to pair you with someone in your college.
Z
#### Not on Co-Op
You cannot be in co-op during the semester that you participate.
Z
#### Status
You must be a full-time master’s student who is enrolled at the Boston campus who has completed at least one semester.
Z
#### Meetings
You will be expected to reach out to connect with mentee(s) at least two to three times during the semester. That’s half the fun! You will be given support and guidance on how to do so.
Z
#### Canvas
You will need to complete a Canvas introductory module by the advertised due date to become a mentor, which you will be enrolled in upon acceptance to the program.
Z
#### Workshops
After completing the introductory Canvas course, you will be required to attend all in-person workshops The first in-person workshop is mandatory for all mentors.
There are two in-person workshop cohorts, which take place on either Thursdays or Fridays from 11:15am-12:30pm. The full workshop schedule is found in the application.
### By completing the following requirements, you will receive a digital badge:
  * Complete the Peer Pathways Program course on Canvas.
  * Provide evidence of reaching out to a mentee to the program facilitator.
  * Confirm that you have checked in with each assigned mentee at least once by the end of the program.
  * Attend the required number of workshops (noted above).
