Program overview 
##  The power of mathematical insight 
The Graduate Certificate in Applied Mathematics at Northeastern University is designed to provide students with foundational knowledge in statistical reasoning, mathematical modeling, and modern mathematical methods. The curriculum includes a blend of theory and applied courses, covering topics from the fundamental structures of mathematical objects to real-world problem-solving with industry partners.
With flexible evening classes and a tailored curriculum, students can balance their studies with professional commitments. The program offers hands-on projects with industry partners, giving students the opportunity to work with real-world data. Graduates of the certificate program gain skills applicable across fields, from finance to technology, and are well-positioned for roles in predictive analytics, data analysis, quantitative research, and more.

Admissions requirements 
  * Online application
  * Unofficial transcripts (official transcripts required upon acceptance of admissions)
    * The Foreign Credential Evaluation (FCE) is a required assessment of all transcripts and documents from non-U.S. accredited post-secondary education institutions. (Review the [FCE requirements](https://northeastern.my.site.com/GraduateAdmissionsFAQ/s/article/What-documents-are-required-for-a-credential-evaluation) by country.)
  * Personal statement
  * English language proficiency:
    * Degree earned or in progress at a U.S institution
    * Degree earned or in progress at an institution where English is the only medium of instruction
    * Official exam scores from either the TOEFL iBT (institution code is 3682), IELTS, PTE exam, or Duolingo English Test. Scores are valid for 2 years from the test date.
