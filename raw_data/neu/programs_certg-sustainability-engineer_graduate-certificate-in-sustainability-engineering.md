Program overview 
##  Help guide sustainability solutions 
The challenges in building a sustainable relationship between civilization and the earth system are inherently complex and multidisciplinary and demand action from nearly every economic sector in the coming decades.
The Graduate Certificate in Sustainability Engineering provides those who have computational literacy but do not have an engineering background with the knowledge and skills to collaborate effectively with engineers.
This certificate provides non-engineering students with the foundational knowledge and technical expertise required to conceive of and participate in multidisciplinary solution development for sustainability.

Unique features 
  * Graduate certificates in the College of Engineering can be taken alongside a master’s degree in the College of Engineering or pursued separately. Students not pursuing a master’s degree who have completed the graduate certificate can apply the certificate credits toward an eligible master’s degree in the College of Engineering.
  * This certificate program is open to students with nonengineering undergraduate degrees.


Career outlook 
Society is increasingly facing highly complex interdisciplinary challenges, resulting in evolving industry needs. Skills that bridge multiple disciplines and a perspective that enables design, implementation, and communication of solutions to complex interdisciplinary challenges is in demand by employers as varied as local and regional governments, environmentally related nongovernmental organizations, and consulting firms, among others.

Admissions requirements 
  * Minimum 3.0 GPA and the completion of an undergraduate degree and coursework covering the fundamentals of basic mathematics, including statistics
  * Complete online application form
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Transcripts from all institutions attended
  * GRE is not required for terms starting during the 2025-2026 academic years
  * Statement of purpose
  * Resumé
  * TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo for international applicants unless the waiver requirements are met
  * The application fee is waived for all new applicants applying to any of our Master of Science or Graduate Certificates for the fall 2025 and spring 2026 application terms.


Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Nonengineering students will gain a foundational knowledge of sustainability engineering, including sustainability challenges from an engineering perspective.
Master in-demand skills 
Understand how environmental chemistry is a key aspect of sustainability challenges and solutions, engineering tools for understanding and addressing sustainability challenges, and data methods.
Improve cross-functional effectiveness 
Gain foundational familiarity with sustainability methods and learn the vocabulary needed to collaborate effectively with engineers.

Degree building 
##  Stack your certificate 
Earning a graduate certificate is a fast and affordable way to acquire specialized skills while building toward your master’s degree. Upon completion of this certificate program, you may be able to apply your credits to an eligible master’s program, saving time and money on your degree.
Some of the eligible master’s programs for this certificate include:
  * [Master of Science in Civil Engineering](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-civil-engr-environmental/master-of-science-in-civil-engineering/)
  * [Master of Science in Sustainable Building Systems](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/mssbs-sustainable-bldg-sys/master-of-science-in-sustainable-building-systems/)
