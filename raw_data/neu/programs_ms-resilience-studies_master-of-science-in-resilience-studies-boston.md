Program overview 
##  Help strengthen communities 
Communities, companies, and countries face serious threats—from terrorism, cyberattacks, and natural hazards to migration, climate change, and pandemics.
The Master of Science in Resilience Studies is a first-of-its-kind graduate degree program that focuses on maximizing systemic and societal resilience. Because you’ll learn from both technical and policy experts, you’ll gain a new perspective on security challenges and the cutting-edge skills needed to prepare for, withstand, and recover rapidly in the face of disasters.
You’ll also be able to choose from industry-aligned electives taught by expert instructors who have track records in resilience work, giving your valuable expertise needed to advance your career and keep communities safe.

Unique features 
  * Choose from flexible part- and full-time options.
  * Learn from and collaborate with respected experts in resilience studies.
  * Help solve real-world problems with local agencies, companies, and nonprofits through collaborative research, Experiential Network (XN) projects, and other opportunities.
  * Build experience and get paid while you earn your master’s with an optional full-time co-op.
  * Network with alums in influential roles at top companies, impactful organizations, and key governmental bodies.


Customize your degree 
Specialize with electives in administration management and policy, counterterrorism and conflict studies, cybersecurity policy, resilient cities, or criminal justice.
Research opportunities 
You can work one-on-one with a faculty member or join a larger research team or lab. Our program works with a number of on-campus research centers, including:
  * Global Resilience Institute
  * Boston Area Research Initiative
  * Center on Crime and Community Resilience
  * Dukakis Center for Urban and Regional Policy
  * Social Impact Lab

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Gain the skills and experience to address dangers and make organizations and society resilient to ever-evolving challenges.
Gain real-world experience 
Help solve real-world problems with local agencies, companies, and nonprofits through collaborative research, Experiential Network projects, and other opportunities.
Interdisciplinary and specialized pathways 
Our resilience studies program focuses on administration management and policy; counterterrorism and conflict studies; cybersecurity policy; resilient cities; and criminal justice.
Create career opportunities 
Our graduates work in the public sector at organizations like FEMA, the U.S. Coast Guard, and Boston City Hall, and in private sector companies including Cadmus Group, United Health Services, and Capital One.
Co-op placements 
You’ll have the option to engage in a six-month full-time work experience through our signature cooperative education (co-op) program, and apply your skills to a diverse array of work environments.
