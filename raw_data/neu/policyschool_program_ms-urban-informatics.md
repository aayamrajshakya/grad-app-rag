#  Master of ScienceUrban Informatics
## Shape the Future of Cities for the Better
In Northeastern’s MS Urban Informatics, you’ll leverage Big Data, modeling, and policy to improve public well-being. This program is accessible to students from technical and nontechnical backgrounds and is offered in Boston, Arlington (VA), and online. It emphasizes democratic and inclusive processes. 
Learn how to use data analytics, statistical and computational modeling, visualization, mapping and spatial analysis, and other applications to address real urban issues. 
## Choose an Action-Oriented Program 
Tackle issues that matter such as the impact of weather extremes, gentrification, and urban accessibility/transportation 
[Focus Areas](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/policyschool/program/ms-urban-informatics/concentrations/)
## Get Hands-On Experience Solving Urban Issues
The MS Urban Informatics program embeds in its courses multiple avenues for experiential learning. The program also offers an optional, full-time, six-month co-op experience. Co-op partners have included The American City Coalition, MBTA, Applied Research in Government Operations, Common Cause, and more. You’ll also have the options to complete a Capstone project and engage in research opportunities. Building a professional portfolio is a part of this program.
[See Co-op + Experience](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/policyschool/program/ms-urban-informatics/experiential-opportunities/)
##  Enjoy MS Urban Informatics Career Results
Be in-demand. 100 percent of alumni find employment within one year of graduation. Graduates work at organizations like Amazon, Ford Motor Company, City of Boston, National Grid, and many more. Many alumni go on to hold leadership roles, driving ethical change. 
[See Career Outcomes](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/policyschool/program/ms-urban-informatics/career-outcomes/)
## Learn From the Best
Work with leading faculty who helped establish the field of urban informatics and participatory modeling

## Flexible. Accessible. High Value.
The 1–2 year STEM-designated MS Urban Informatics is one of the best and most flexible in the field. 
  * Open to students with and without a technical background
  * Available 100% online, hybrid, or in person (Boston or Arlington)
  * Full time or part time
  * Automatic consideration for scholarships up to 50% off tuition
