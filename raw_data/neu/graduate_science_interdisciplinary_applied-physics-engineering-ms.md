## [2025-2026 Edition](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/)
The combined MS program in applied physics and engineering allows graduate students to receive training in one of three concentrations of the electrical and computer engineering department while also receiving fundamental graduate-level physics training that is relevant to that area.
## Thesis Option
Students may register for an additional two semesters of thesis work. Depending on the affiliation of the thesis advisor, students may register for Thesis (PHYS 7990)Master’s Project (EECE 7945)Thesis (EECE 7990)
Complete all courses and requirements listed below unless otherwise indicated.
## Concentrations
Complete one of the following concentrations:
  * [Analysis, Modeling, and Computation](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/graduate/science/physics/applied-physics-engineering-ms/#analysis)
  * [Electromagnetics, Plasma, and Optics](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/graduate/science/physics/applied-physics-engineering-ms/#elpo)
  * [Microsystems, Materials, and Devices](https://catalog.northeastern.edu/graduate/science/physics/applied-physics-engineering-ms/#Micro)
