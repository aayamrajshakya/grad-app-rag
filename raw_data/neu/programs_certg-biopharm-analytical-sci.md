Program overview 
##  Biopharmaceutical mastery 
The Graduate Certificate in Biopharmaceutical Analytical Sciences addresses the biotechnology industry’s need for professionals with advanced skills in protein analysis. The program provides comprehensive training on state-of-the-art analytical techniques for characterizing biopharmaceuticals, including both innovator and biosimilar products.
Through four graduate-level courses, students gain hands-on experience and practical skills, enhancing their ability to contribute effectively within the biotech sector. Ideal for those currently working in biotechnology or those seeking to enter the field, this certificate can also serve as a pathway to the MS in Biotechnology program, with all 12 credits eligible for transfer.

Curriculum highlights 
  * Four graduate-level courses: Includes core topics like biopharmaceutical design, quality assurance, glycoprotein analysis, and protein mass spectrometry.
  * 12 total credits: The curriculum is designed to provide comprehensive knowledge within a short timeframe.
  * Stackable credential: All 12 credits can be applied toward the MS in Biotechnology program.

Admissions requirements

(Review the [FCE requirements](https://northeastern.my.site.com/GraduateAdmissionsFAQ/s/article/What-documents-are-required-for-a-credential-evaluation) by country.)

  * Personal statement
  * English language proficiency:
    * Degree earned or in progress at a U.S. institution
    * Degree earned or in progress at an institution where English is the only medium of instruction
    * Official exam scores from either the TOEFL iBT (institution code is 3682), IELTS, PTE exam, or Duolingo English Test. Scores are valid for 2 years from the test date.
