Program overview 
##  Mental healthcare with heart 
The online Master of Science in Nursing-Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner program is designed to prepare nurses to offer evidence-based, compassionate mental health services to individuals, families, and communities. This hybrid program blends flexible online coursework with two in-person Clinical Intensive weekends in Boston.
Through comprehensive training in psychopharmacology, diagnostic decision-making, and therapeutic interventions, students gain the knowledge needed to address psychiatric issues across the lifespan. Graduates are eligible to sit for the Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner certification exam.

Accreditations 
The master’s degree program in nursing and the post-graduate APRN certificate program at Northeastern University are accredited by the Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education, 655 K Street, NW, Suite 750, Washington, DC 20001, 202-887-6791.
Program highlights 
  * Hybrid format with online classes and two hands-on Clinical Intensive weekends on the Boston campus.
  * Clinical practica throughout the program provides real-world mental health care experience.
  * Prepares graduates to sit for the national PMHNP certification exam.
  * Learn from experienced faculty who are leaders in psychiatric and mental health care.
  * Flexible study options allow you to balance coursework with your professional and personal life.


Career outlook 
This program equips graduates to serve as psychiatric nurse practitioners in diverse settings, such as hospitals, behavioral health clinics, and private practices. PMHNPs are trained to assess, diagnose, and treat mental health conditions, providing therapeutic care and managing treatment plans for patients of all ages. This credential opens the door to advanced practice roles with greater responsibility and potential for career growth.
Experiential learning 
Northeastern’s signature experience-powered learning model has been a cornerstone of the university for over a century. By blending rigorous academics with hands-on professional practice, you’ll gain real-world skills that can be immediately applied in your workplace. This dynamic approach transforms your education into a meaningful, career-advancing experience, offering countless opportunities for personal and professional growth. Northeastern prepares you not just for a degree but for impactful success in your field.

Admissions requirements 
  * Application.
  * Application fee.
  * Three letters of recommendation (academic and professional).
  * Personal statement.
  * Resumé.
  * Transcripts from all institutions attended.
  * Baccalaureate degree in nursing from an accredited USA program.
  * Minimum GPA of 3.0, with grades of ‘B’ or better, is required in all undergraduate clinical courses.
  * Successful completion of a statistics course that includes probability theory and hypothesis testing.
  * Current RN licensure.
  * At least 1 year of active full-time nursing experience with direct patient care.
  * TOEFL or IELTS for applicants who do not hold a degree from a U.S. institution and whose native language is not English.
  * Additional fees may apply for travel, memberships, and clinical.
