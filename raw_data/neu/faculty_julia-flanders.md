#  JuliaFlanders
Professor of the Practice and Director, Digital Scholarship Group 
Julia Flanders teaches courses in digital humanities and co-administers Northeastern’s [graduate certificate in Digital Humanities](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/nulab/program/dh-certificate/). She directs the [Centers for Digital Scholarship](https://cds.library.northeastern.edu/), the [Digital Scholarship Group](https://dsg.northeastern.edu/), and the [Women Writers Project](https://www.wwp.northeastern.edu/), and serves as editor in chief of [Digital Humanities Quarterly](https://dhq.digitalhumanities.org/), an open-access, peer-reviewed online journal of digital humanities. She was involved in the early development of digital humanities initiatives including the [Text Encoding Initiative](https://www.tei-c.org/) and the [Alliance of Digital Humanities Organizations](https://adho.org/). She has served as chair of the TEI Consortium and as President of the [Association for Computers and the Humanities](https://ach.org/). She has also taught a wide range of workshops on text encoding and served as a consultant and advisor on numerous digital humanities projects, including the [Orlando Project](https://www.theorlandoproject.org/), [The Recovery Hub for American Women Writers](https://recoveryhub.siue.edu/), and the [Walt Whitman Archive](https://whitmanarchive.org/). Her research interests focus on data modeling, textual scholarship, humanities data curation, and sustainable digital project management. She is the co-editor, with Neil Fraistat, of The Cambridge Companion to Textual ScholarshipThe Shape of Data in Digital Humanities: Modeling Texts and Text-based Resources
