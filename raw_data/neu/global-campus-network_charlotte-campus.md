## Reimagining healthcare for all
As one of the nation’s fastest-growing cities, Charlotte is in need of committed professionals who are ready to lead the way in their industries. Northeastern students benefit from partnerships with local colleges and universities, businesses, health systems, and nonprofits, allowing them to gain real health and life sciences experience that improves careers and communities.
##  What makes Charlotte unique 
As an emerging center of healthcare, Charlotte is the perfect place for Northeastern students interested in medicine to make an impact in their communities.
  * [ Visit the campus website](https://charlotte.northeastern.edu/)


Top-tier research 
Northeastern is the only R1 research university in Charlotte. The classification is the highest available to universities and highlights Northeastern’s commitment to relevant and technologically advanced research.
Community-focused 
In addition to hosting the pro bono [Speech-Language Center](https://charlotte.northeastern.edu/about-us/community-engagement/speech-language-center/) on campus, Northeastern holds community events, like Local Leaders Global Impact and the Heart of Charlotte Awards, to support neighbors.
## Fast facts: Charlotte's job market
Charlotte's economy has seen significant growth in healthcare, entrepreneurship, and the life sciences, giving our graduates plenty of opportunities to find the right role. 
97% Percent of Northeastern's Charlotte-based students employed within 12 months of graduation
4.6% Annual employment growth in Charlotte in 2023 
(U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2023)
38% Life science business growth in North Carolina from 2018 to 2022
(NC Biotech, 2023)
##  The Center for Health Sciences 
The [Center for Health Sciences](https://charlotte.northeastern.edu/center-for-health-sciences/) improves health for all so that people can live longer, more fulfilling lives, even with disease or disability. Partners include local and regional companies, nonprofits, and policymakers.
##  Charlotte campus highlights 
Students based in Charlotte benefit from the city’s strong connections with healthcare and life sciences employers, a growing job market, and increasing investments in local businesses.
  * [ Learn more about studying in Charlotte](https://charlotte.northeastern.edu/about-us/campus-and-location/)


A focus on the life sciences 
Northeastern seeks to build the next generation of health science innovators and patient-centered practitioners in partnership with community health systems, businesses, and nonprofits.
A community-based inclusive approach 
We strive to prepare a diverse group of future health science practitioners to serve disadvantaged communities through community partnerships, experience-based learning, and pro bono work.
One of the nation's fastest-growing cities 
Charlotte is one of the nation’s fastest-growing cities, with significant investments and career opportunities in the life and health sciences sectors.
