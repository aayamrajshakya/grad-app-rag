#  Science Connects to Innovation Program 
The Science Connects to Innovation program empowers undergraduate science students to create entrepreneurial solutions, spanning cleaner energy, eco-friendly pesticides, potential anticancer compounds, sustainable foods, and high-tech assistive devices. 
[apply for the scholarship](https://forms.office.com/Pages/ResponsePage.aspx?id=gcLuqKOqrk2sm5o5i5IV5zRnqAcIWZpBkEhqlgOod9xURjBLMkxTOEtGRVk0MTRLRk5KVTFVRFpSOS4u)
Science Connects to Innovation aims to inspire College of Science students to engage with Northeastern’s venture creation network, offering support for recognizing and pursuing entrepreneurial opportunities. The program also provides essential resources for translating their ideas into ventures, encompassing prototyping, marketing, and more.
## Science Connects to Innovation Scholarship
About the scholarship 
## About the scholarship
  
The Science Connects to Innovation Scholarship is a $5,000 one-time award for full-time COS undergraduate students. Applicants must be housed in the COS graduating no sooner than Spring 2026. Scholarship recipients are encouraged to use the funds to expand opportunities for entrepreneurial learning and development, and promote entrepreneurship to undergraduate students in the College of Science.

## Application requirements

  * Current CV/resumé

## Ada AI Chatbot
In this Entrepreneurship Speakers Series talk, Dr. Abby Williams, Dr. Nik Brown, and Dev Shah presented Ada, an AI chatbot they designed to assist students with Calculus. Ada provides on-demand support to help students navigate complex mathematical concepts, answering questions and offering explanations tailored to their needs.

## Husky Startup Challenge
Each semester, the Husky Startup Challenge guides student entrepreneurs through boot camps focused on venture creation—from ideation to prototyping. The program culminates in Demo Day, where founders pitch their ideas in two-minute presentations to a live audience.
In fall 2023, Alexis Musaelyan-Blackmon, a data science and biology student won first place and a cash prize of $4,500 for her business pitch of Exploro—an online platform to help academic researchers recruit participants for their studies.
[Learn more](https://news.northeastern.edu/2023/12/01/startup-challenge/)
