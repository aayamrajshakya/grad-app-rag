Program overview 
##  Turn ideas into market success 
The Graduate Certificate in Entrepreneurship at Northeastern University equips you with the essential skills to bring technological products from concept to launch. In just four or five master’s-level courses, you’ll learn from experienced entrepreneurs and study real-world cases that bring theory to life.
The curriculum covers areas like venture financing, technology application, and business planning, with elective options such as lean design, operations management, or marketing to tailor your experience to your career goals. This flexible program, available both full-time and part-time, also offers access to experiential learning opportunities that enrich your perspective and build confidence.

Accreditations 
Accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
Program highlights 
  * Personalize your curriculum with electives that align with your career goals.
  * Create a comprehensive business plan applicable in your current role or for your own startup.
  * Study on-campus in Boston, a global hub for business and innovation.
  * Access guidance from the D’Amore-McKim Graduate Career Center.
  * Explore experiential opportunities like the DATA Club or IDEA, Northeastern’s student-led venture accelerator.
  * Lay the groundwork for a future MBA by applying your certificate credits toward an eligible master’s degree.
  * Qualify for Optional Practical Training (OPT) as an international student through full-time study.


Career outlook 
Mastering new business skills is crucial for advancing your career, whether in a corporate setting or launching your own venture. The Graduate Certificate in Entrepreneurship equips you with up-to-date knowledge, setting the stage for roles in startup investment or laying the foundation to launch your enterprise.
Experiential learning 
Northeastern’s experiential learning model focuses on real-world application. Your courses will include case studies and project work, allowing you to learn from successful companies and refine your skills in a collaborative setting. Enhance your business knowledge through unique opportunities, such as:
  * DATA Club
  * Data KIPS
  * IDEA, Northeastern’s student-led venture accelerator

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * One letter of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)

Degree building 
##  Stack your certificate 
Earning a graduate certificate is a fast and affordable way to acquire specialized skills while building toward your master’s degree. Upon completion of this certificate program, you may be able to apply your credits to an eligible master’s program, saving time and money on your degree.
Some of the eligible master’s programs for this certificate include:
  * [Master of Science in Management](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/ms-management/master-of-science-in-management/)
  * [Part-time MBA](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/mba-business-admin-evening/part-time-mba/)
  * [Master of Science in Finance/MBA – Part-Time](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/msfmba-finance-business-evenin/master-of-science-in-finance-mba-part-time/)


Contact an enrollment counselor to learn more about eligibility requirements.
