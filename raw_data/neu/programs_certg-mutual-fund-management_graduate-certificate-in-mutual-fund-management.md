Program overview 
##  The world of portfolio management 
Northeastern’s Graduate Certificate in Mutual Fund Management prepares you to excel in building and managing investment portfolios. In as few as four courses, you’ll develop advanced quantitative and analytical skills essential for driving value in today’s competitive financial markets.
Dive into market analysis, compliance, and ethics while personalizing your learning with electives like investment analysis or portfolio management. Gain real-world investment experience through the 360 Huntington Fund and access expert career guidance. Study part-time on our Boston campus with evening classes designed to fit your schedule.

Accreditations 
Accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
Program highlights 
  * Become proficient in quantitative and analytical methods for effective financial management.
  * Tailor your learning with electives aligned to your career goals, including topics like fixed income securities and portfolio management.
  * Gain real-world investment experience by joining the 360 Huntington Fund, Northeastern’s student-managed mutual fund.
  * Study part-time with flexible evening classes on our Boston campus.
  * Access career support from Northeastern’s Employer Engagement and Career Design office.
  * Earn credits that can be applied toward a future master’s degree at Northeastern.


Future potential 
Ideal for finance professionals looking to advance, this certificate enhances your strategic skills and expertise in mutual fund management. Graduates can leverage their knowledge for higher-level roles, increased salary potential, or career shifts within the financial sector.
Experiential learning 
Experience is key to mastering mutual fund management. At Northeastern, you’ll engage in applied projects, study real-world business cases, and have the unique opportunity to contribute to the 360 Huntington Fund. Learn directly from faculty who are seasoned practitioners in investing and financial strategy, equipping you with insights you can apply immediately in the finance industry.

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * One letter of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)

##  Stack your certificate 
Earning a graduate certificate is a fast and affordable way to acquire specialized skills while building toward your master’s degree. Upon completion of this certificate program, you may be able to apply your credits to an eligible master’s program, saving time and money on your degree.
