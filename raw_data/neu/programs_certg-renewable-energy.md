Program overview 
##  Drive the future of renewables 
Northeastern University’s Graduate Certificate in Renewable Energy equips you with the skills to analyze and implement renewable energy systems. This four-course program dives deep into solar and wind technologies, energy systems engineering, and environmental regulations like the LEED certification program.
You’ll also explore the EPA’s regulatory framework and learn about industrial ecology, including life cycle analysis and cost modeling. Designed to provide practical experience, this certificate is ideal for those looking to advance or transition into the growing renewable energy sector.

Career outlook 
With the growing demand for renewable energy solutions, this certificate offers specialized skills that companies value. It serves as a gateway to new career opportunities in green energy, where certificate holders often see a significant salary boost.
Experiential learning 
Northeastern’s signature experiential learning model integrates academics with real-world practice. Engage in cooperative education (co-op) placements, where you’ll work from four to eight months in diverse organizations, from large companies to innovative startups. Benefit from professional development resources, including resumé building and interview preparation, to jumpstart your career in renewable energy.
