Program overview 
##  Apply AI to your industry 
Our Master of Professional Studies in Applied Machine Intelligence is a multidisciplinary, experience-based program that prepares you to innovate within the rapidly growing field of artificial intelligence (AI). Through courses and projects that focus on applied machine learning, AI, and data management, analysis, and visualization, you’ll learn to address business issues in any industry.
Students take on Experiential Network (XN) projects that partner them with businesses around the world—including Google, Deloitte, Samsung, Uber, and more—to solve pressing challenges with AI and other technologies. These projects teach students how to collaborate, communicate, ask meaningful questions, and effectively solve problems while demonstrating to employers that they have the technical skills necessary to succeed.

Unique features 
  * Graduate with a portfolio of work samples that demonstrate your range and depth of competencies and skills with machine learning and artificial intelligence.
  * Work closely with faculty from different fields and areas of expertise.
  * Our experiential AI curricula includes an introductory core, as well as an advanced core with an end-to-end AI education, with the goal to proactively and thoughtfully prepare students for the evolving technology and the challenges it presents.
  * Target a successful career in applied artificial intelligence and machine learning.
  * Prepare to excel with the highest in-demand skills focusing on data, technology, and human literacy.
  * Leverage the program’s modular curriculum to customize your classes, projects, and other experiential learning opportunities based on your unique interests.
  * Choose to specialize your studies with concentrations in healthcare, finance, human resources, and business ventures.

Admissions requirements 
  * Online application
  * Professional resumé
  * Unofficial undergraduate transcripts; official transcripts required at the time of admission
    * The Foreign Credential Evaluation (FCE) is a required assessment of all transcripts and documents from non-U.S. accredited post-secondary education institutions. (Review the [FCE requirements](https://northeastern.my.site.com/GraduateAdmissionsFAQ/s/article/What-documents-are-required-for-a-credential-evaluation) by country.)

    * Official associate or bachelor's degree transcript from an accredited college or university in the U.S., stating degree conferral and date
    * TOEFL, IELTS, PTE, Duolingo, or NU Global Exam scores
  * Other materials may be required 


Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Develop your skills in three key literacies of machine learning and artificial intelligence.
Technological literacy 
Evaluate and integrate tools, techniques, and conceptual skills necessary for managing complex systems.
Data literacy 
Illustrate the significance of digital and global transformation to create business strategies, make data-informed decisions, and drive innovation as well as create a competitive advantage.
Human literacy 
Develop lifelong learning, communication, design, and innovation leadership skills needed to guide AI systems, including assessing and developing an ethical position, critical thinking, and cultural agility.
