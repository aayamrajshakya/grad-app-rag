The Graduate Certificate in Informatics provides you with an opportunity to learn and improve information technology, technical, and management skills. Our curriculum focuses on IT strategy and governance, interactive system design and web development, and cloud computing applications and management.
This interdisciplinary program blends computer science, data analytics, and domain-specific knowledge across healthcare, business, education, government, and nonprofit organizations. You’ll gain a unique skill set that bridges technical expertise with practical applications, preparing you for leadership roles in our technology-driven world.
Technology continues to be a driving force in our world, fueling a high demand for professionals who can skillfully use technology to solve problems. Opportunities in information technology and computing remain strong, with many jobs offering competitive salaries.
#### Key growth areas:
  * Information security analysts
  * Computer and information systems managers
  * Computer and information research scientists

For students interested in pursuing financial assistance or educational loans, additional educational costs, known as Cost of Attendance (COA) components, can be included in the calculation of aid and loan eligibility. Components may include food, housing, books, course materials, supplies, equipment, transportation, personal expenses, and the cost of obtaining a first professional licensure.
