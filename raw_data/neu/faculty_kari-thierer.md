## Biography
Dr. Thierer is a faculty member in the Graduate School of Education. She teaches in both the Masters in Education program and the Doctorate in Education program, as well as chairing dissertation committees.
**Programs Affiliated:** M.Ed – Higher Education; EdD
**Teaching and Research Focus Area:**  
Dr. Thierer has expertise in curriculum design, assessment, and evaluation. She also has a research interest in supporting neurodiverse students, particularly as they transition from high school to college. Additionally, she is a subject matter expert for the Department of Labor supporting their Strengthening Community Colleges grantees. She also serves as a reviewer for the grants for the Department of Education and the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services’ Administration for Children and Families.
**Professional Background:**  
Dr. Thierer began her career as a K-12 teacher with a specialty in career and technical education. She has served as a national director for a nonprofit network of schools, as well as an executive director for a national nonprofit that focused on school reform and equity. She was the associate dean for teaching, learning, and assessment at a community college in Washington State where she also ran the teaching and learning center. She has been at Northeastern since 2022.
**Academic Background:** Dr. Thierer has a Bachelor’s degree in English from the University of Washington, Seattle, a Masters in Educational Administration from the University of Phoenix, and a Doctorate in Education from Northeastern University.
