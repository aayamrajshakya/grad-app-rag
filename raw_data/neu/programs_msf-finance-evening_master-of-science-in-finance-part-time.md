Program overview 
##  Shape the future of finance 
Level up your finance knowledge and prepare to lead in a dynamic, fast-paced industry. The MS in Finance immerses you in the latest trends in financial theory and practice, preparing you for a career with exciting opportunities for advancement. You can study at a pace that suits you, fitting your classes around work and life commitments.
Your coursework will help you pursue the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation. You’ll study the essentials of value creation, investment strategy, and market analysis—adding electives that take you deeper into topics that align with your goals. Your faculty will include financial thought leaders and entrepreneurs who teach through the lens of their own experience.
Dive into the [MS in Finance](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/programs/msf-finance-evening/) on the D’Amore-McKim School of Business website.

Program features 
  * Prepare to excel in the future of finance—an industry being reshaped by rapid technological advances.
  * Master financial best practices and how to apply them in a variety of settings.
  * Apply to gain experience in equity research and portfolio management as an analyst for the D’Amore-McKim student-managed mutual fund, the [360 Huntington Fund](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/360-huntington-fund/).
  * Deepen your understanding of business operations in the global economy through the [Bloomberg Certification](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/bloomberg-business-lab/bloomberg-certifications/#bmc) process with Bloomberg Market Concepts (BMC) and Bloomberg Environmental, Social, & Governance (ESG) certifications.
  * Develop a global mindset through sharing your experiences with faculty and classmates in financial services.
  * If you want to pursue the Chartered Financial Analyst designation, your coursework will help you prepare for the exams.
  * You’ll be able to adjust your schedule each semester and take one or multiple classes per week.


Career outlook 
In the D’Amore-McKim MS in Finance program, you’ll add advanced financial expertise and skills to your current industry experience, positioning you to pursue more significant roles. Depending on your background, you may be able to leverage your new degree into a position as a wealth manager, mutual fund analyst, financial consultant, or investment banker—well-paid, highly sought-after careers that offer the potential to move toward executive-level leadership in finance.
Our graduates are heavily recruited by companies of all sizes, from startups to multinational organizations.

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)
