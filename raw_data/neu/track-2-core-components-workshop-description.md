#### Workshop Descriptions
Workshop 1: Research Integrity Foundations: Ethics, Compliance, and Best Practices (October 8, 2025)
Please note this workshop was held on Wednesday, October 8, 2025
**Location:** Curry Student Center Room #346
**Workshop Details:**
Research integrity forms the foundation of credible, impactful scholarship. This comprehensive in-person workshop brings together expertise from NU-RES Compliance, NU-RES Finance, and the Center for Research Innovation to provide researchers with essential knowledge across all dimensions of responsible research conduct. Through case-based scenarios and interactive discussions, participants will explore the interconnected nature of research ethics, financial stewardship, and operational excellence.
The workshop addresses critical areas including research misconduct, questionable research practices, grant stewardship responsibilities, conflict of interest management, research budget oversight, and professional conduct standards. Real-world case studies will illustrate how these components work together to create a robust framework for research integrity, while highlighting the consequences when systems break down.
This intensive session is designed for researchers at all career stages who want to strengthen their understanding of research responsibility and develop practical tools for maintaining the highest standards of integrity in their work. The in-person format encourages collaborative learning and provides opportunities for detailed discussion of complex scenarios with compliance and administrative experts.
**Learning Objectives:**
By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:
  1. Distinguish
  2. Apply
  3. Evaluate
  4. Implement
  5. Integrate
  6. Design
  7. Analyze


Workshop 2: Leading with Purpose: Research Team Management and Support (February 4, 2026)
**Location:** TBD
**Workshop Details:**
Effective research leadership extends far beyond scientific expertise—it requires building inclusive environments where teams thrive, communication flows effectively, and every member can contribute their best work. This comprehensive in-person workshop brings together experts from the Ombuds Offices, OUEC, the Marine Science Center, the College of Social Sciences and Humanities, and the College of Engineering to address the multifaceted challenges of leading research teams.
Through interactive case studies and facilitated discussions, participants will explore essential leadership competencies including mentoring and coaching strategies, team building approaches, creating safe and inclusive work environments, managing complex professional relationships, and promoting wellness for both leaders and team members. Real-world scenarios will illustrate how these elements interconnect to create productive, supportive research cultures where innovation flourishes.
This workshop serves both emerging research leaders taking on their first management responsibilities and experienced faculty seeking to deepen their leadership skills. The interactive format encourages participants to share their own insights and challenges, creating a collaborative learning environment that benefits from diverse perspectives and experiences across disciplines and career stages.
**Learning Objectives:**
By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:
  1. Implement
  2. Design
  3. Create
  4. Navigate
  5. Develop
  6. Integrate


Workshop 3: Contemporary Ethical Issues in Complex Research Environments (February 25, 2026)
**Location:** TBD
**Workshop Details:**
The research landscape has grown increasingly complex as institutions navigate shifting federal policies, evolving executive orders, and heightened scrutiny of international collaborations. These pressures create unprecedented ethical dilemmas for researchers, administrators, and staff across all disciplines, while simultaneously highlighting the critical importance and benefits of international collaboration for advancing knowledge and addressing global challenges. This timely in-person workshop creates space for informed dialogue.
Featuring speakers from the College of Science, the College of Social Sciences and Humanities, and the College of Arts, Media, and Design, this workshop will offer diverse disciplinary perspectives on contemporary ethical challenges. Through facilitated discussion and philosophical exploration, participants will examine critical issues including international collaboration in challenging geopolitical contexts, conducting collaborative science under external pressure, fulfilling responsibilities as engaged members of society, and preserving academic freedom while ensuring ethical compliance. The workshop acknowledges the real anxieties felt throughout the university community and encourages reflective thinking about navigating these tensions.
This session will be of broad interest to members of the Northeastern community—faculty, researchers, and staff—regardless of their direct involvement in research activities. The current environment affects everyone from faculty to students to staff, making shared understanding of these issues valuable for institutional resilience and continued excellence in research and education.
**Learning Objectives:**
By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:
  1. Evaluate
  2. Reflect
  3. Examine
  4. Identify
  5. Navigate
