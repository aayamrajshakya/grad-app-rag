Program overview 
##  Master accounting and finance 
Successful businesses need professionals who can analyze data, assess risk, and set sound financial strategies. With Northeastern’s Graduate Certificate in Accounting and Financial Decision Making, you’ll develop these critical skills quickly in just four or five master’s-level courses.
Your core coursework covers topics like financial reporting, portfolio management, and information analysis, providing a framework for strategic decision-making. Personalize your curriculum by choosing electives aligned with your career goals, such as global competition or information management.
Learn in-person at our Boston campus, and gain hands-on experience through opportunities like managing a real mutual fund in Northeastern’s endowment.

Career outlook 
As artificial intelligence and technology speed up the pace of business, learning new skills-and refreshing old ones-will be critical to advancing your career. Earning your Graduate Certificate in Accounting and Financial Decision Making equips you with the specialized, up-to-the-moment knowledge that companies value and demonstrates your commitment to professional development.
The accounting and financial skills you’ll gain will translate to almost any industry, serving as a stepping stone toward an analyst, managerial, or specialist role. You can be confident that your credential from D’Amore-McKim carries weight in the business community. Employers appreciate our focus on the digital economy, which is one reason our graduates are highly recruited by top firms, multinational businesses, and startups.
Graduates can leverage Northeastern’s global network of employer partners, offering unique opportunities to connect, innovate, and scale your career locally and globally.

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * One letter of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)
