#  DanielFaber
Professor of Sociology 
Daniel Faber is a Professor of Sociology at Northeastern University, and a Senior Research Fellow at the Global Center for Climate Justice. He is also the Co-Editor in Chief of the journal Capitalism, Nature, Socialism. Environment Under Fire: Imperialism and the Ecological Crisis in Central AmericaChoice Magazine The Struggle for Ecological Democracy: Environmental Justice Movements in the United StatesCapitalizing on Environmental Justice: The Polluter-Industrial Complex in the Age of GlobalizationFoundations for Social Change: Critical Perspectives on Philanthropy and Popular MovementsThe Ecological Contradictions of American Capitalism: Towards a More Transformative Environmental Politics.
[View CV](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/1-19-24-Faber-vitae.pdf)
Awards & Honors
In 2006, Dr. Faber received the “Champion for Justice Award,” granted by the Alliance for a Healthy Tomorrow (AHT), and the “Friend of the Earth and Environmental Justice Award” from Salem State College and HealthLink for his “path-breaking leadership and work in Environmental Justice in Massachusetts and beyond.” In 2010 he received the Environmental Sociology Practice and Outreach Award from the Environmental Sociology and Technology Section of the American Sociological Association. He has also received recognition for the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP), the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), and many other organizations for his work in advancing a more transformative environmental justice politics.
