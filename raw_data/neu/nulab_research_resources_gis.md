# GIS and Visualization
A GIS visualization from “[The Birth of Boston](https://bostonresearchcenter.org/projects/birth-of-boston/)” project. Image source: Massachusetts Historical Society
This page has frameworks and guidelines for designing and creating visualizations and working with geographic data for analysis. 
### Tools and Tutorials
GIS and Visualization Tutorials
  * [Northeastern Library Subject Guide](https://subjectguides.lib.neu.edu/c.php?g=1019498&p=7384780) – Guides to using spatial datasets, GIS, and other visualization software. Curated by the Northeastern Library Senior Data Analyst Bahare Sanaie-Movahed.
  * [QGIS Tutorials](https://courses.spatialthoughts.com/introduction-to-qgis.html) – A collection of tutorials for using the open-source GIS software QGIS from basic introductions to advanced tools. Written by Ujaval Gandhi.


Mapping and Visualization Tools
  * [Carto](https://carto.com/) – A proprietary web spatial analysis and mapping platform with free student trials. See _[Becoming a Spatial Data Scientist](https://web.northeastern.edu/nulab/wp-content/uploads/2019/11/Ebook-Becoming-Spatial-Data-Scientist.pdf), _an E-book guide on how to use Carto mapping software, to learn more.
  * [Open Source GIS Tools](https://subjectguides.lib.neu.edu/c.php?g=1019498&p=7384791) – Links to free, open-source GIS software like Teach GIS, GRASS, and Quantum GIS, among many others.
  * [SketchUp](https://www.sketchup.com/?gclid=EAIaIQobChMI_suDkdz55QIVrYFaBR2KEQ15EAAYASAAEgKk7fD_BwE) – 3D Modelling Software for Architecture, Interior Design, and Spatial Visualizations.


R Tutorials
  * [Drawing Beautiful Maps Programmatically with R, sf, and ggplot2](https://www.r-spatial.org/r/2018/10/25/ggplot2-sf.html) – An R tutorial that teaches how to draw maps in R using the popular sfggplot2
  * [Introduction to Visualizing Spatial Data in R](https://cran.r-project.org/doc/contrib/intro-spatial-rl.pdf) – An article on spatial data in R. Written by Robin Lovelace, et al.
  * [Making Maps with R](https://geocompr.robinlovelace.net/adv-map.html) – A chapter of Geocomputation with R
  * [Making Maps with R](https://eriqande.github.io/rep-res-web/lectures/making-maps-with-R.html) – Material from a “Reproducible Research Course” on making maps in R. Written by Eric C. Anderson of NOAA/SWFSC.


Tableau Tutorials
  * [Create a Map with Tableau](http://miriamposner.com/classes/dh201w19/tutorials-guides/mapping/create-a-map-with-tableau/) – A tutorial on how to build basic maps, with multiple options for filtering. Written by Miriam Posner of UCLA.
  * [How to Build an Interactive County-Level Map in Tableau](http://www.storybench.org/build-interactive-county-level-map-tableau/) – A tutorial on how to make an interactive map in Tableau, published on [Storybench](https://www.storybench.org/), a “cookbook for digital storytelling” produced by Northeastern’s School of Journalism.
  * [3 Ways to Make Magnificent Maps in Tableau](https://playfairdata.com/3-ways-to-make-magnificent-maps-in-tableau/) – Tutorials on using Tableau to make dual-axis maps, integrating with MapBox, and adding data at different levels of analysis. Written by Ryan Sleeper of PlayfairData.


### Data
Datasets
  * [Boston Data Portal](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/bari/boston-data-portal/) – Mapping data provided by the Boston Area Research Initiative (BARI).
  * [Boston Housing Data](https://www.kaggle.com/c/boston-housing) – Mapping data specific to Boston and its suburbs.
  * [GIS Datasets](https://subjectguides.lib.neu.edu/gis/usadatasets) – Links to a variety of GIS datasets collected on the Northeastern Library’s GIS subject guide.
  * [GIS Shapefiles Database](https://freegisdata.rtwilson.com/) – A repository of mapping data from a variety of sources and agencies.
  * [United States Census Data](https://www2.census.gov/) – Geocoded data from the United States Census Bureau.
  * [USGS GIS Data](https://www.usgs.gov/products/data-and-tools/gis-data) – Mapping data provided by the United States Geological Survey.
