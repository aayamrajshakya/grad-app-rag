Program overview 
##  Innovative physical therapy training 
Northeastern University physical therapy graduates are innovative, global leaders who excel in clinical practice, research, and community service. Our students graduate with exceptional clinical decision-making skills and experience in the field of physical therapy.
If you hold a baccalaureate degree in another field of study, the Post Baccalaureate Doctor of Physical Therapy program (PB-DPT) may be right for you. With an earned undergraduate degree and the completion of the outlined core prerequisites, you may apply as a graduate student to the professional phase of our DPT program.
For the cohort entering in 2026, please note this academic program is not set up to support students, who require F-1 non-immigrant status, to study within the United States.

Program benefits 
  * The program is appropriate for those with a bachelor’s degree in another field
  * The program includes knowledge-based, cooperative, and clinical education components
  * Our DPT builds on the university’s core values of interdisciplinary education, urban engagement, international knowledge, and cutting-edge research

Admissions requirements 
  * PTCAS application
  * Three letters of recommendation
  * Transcripts from all institutions attended
  * Personal statement
  * TOEFL or IELTS for applicants who do not hold a degree from a U.S. institution and whose native language is not English
  * Undergraduate and Prerequisite GPA 3.00 (B) or higher
  * Online labs are accepted
  * The following prerequisites:
    * Satisfactory completion with a grade of C or better within 7 years of all science courses is required:
      * Anatomy & Physiology with Lab (2 Courses, 3-4 Credits each) 
      * Chemistry 1 with Lab (4 Credits) 
      * Chemistry 2 (3-4 Credits) 
      * Physics with Lab (2 Courses, 4 Credits each) 
      * Exercise Physiology (3-4 Credits)
    * Satisfactory completion with a grade of C or better within 10 years of the following prerequisites:
      * General Psychology (3-4 Credits)
      * Higher Level Psychology Course (3-4 Credits)
      * Statistics (3-4 Credits)


Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
DPT students receive knowledge and training experience for professional success in physical therapy.
Learn from expert physical therapists 
Faculty are active in the American Physical Therapy Association and engaged in active clinical research and practice. They bring relevant insights to the classroom and provide mentorship to students.
Integrate experiential and didactic learning 
Learn through the use of standardized patients, communication and interaction with community consultants, participation in service-learning projects, and engagement in research with our faculty.
