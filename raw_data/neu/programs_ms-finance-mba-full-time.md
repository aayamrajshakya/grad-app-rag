## Applicant FAQs
How do I apply and what will I need? 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our[ ](https://nam12.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=https%3A%2F%2Fenroll.northeastern.edu%2Fapply%2F&data=05%7C02%7Cr.higgins%40northeastern.edu%7Ce1a77e86cffb49d64bdf08ddd3b16ad8%7Ca8eec281aaa34daeac9b9a398b9215e7%7C0%7C0%7C638899481133232431%7CUnknown%7CTWFpbGZsb3d8eyJFbXB0eU1hcGkiOnRydWUsIlYiOiIwLjAuMDAwMCIsIlAiOiJXaW4zMiIsIkFOIjoiTWFpbCIsIldUIjoyfQ%3D%3D%7C0%7C%7C%7C&sdata=QvZSDvmKy2qgQIHl3jPFRZZp6GZccCvlAijBW1ATrlA%3D&reserved=0)[application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You'll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Virtual interview (by invitation only)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)


[Learn more about applying to D'Amore-McKim](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/how-to-apply/)
Are there other application requirements for international students? 
Yes, international students must meet additional requirements, including proof of English language proficiency, a valid passport, and English translations of academic transcripts. Our [international graduate students page](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/faq/international-graduate-students/) provides more information about applying and the benefits of choosing D'Amore-McKim. 
Are there opportunities for international students to gain work experience in the United States? 
Yes. International students in F-1 immigration status are eligible for a corporate residency using [Curricular Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/f-1-curricular-practical-training/) authorization. When you complete your program, you may become eligible to apply for [Optional Practical Training](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/off-campus-employment/applying-for-post-completion-opt-f-1/) (OPT), which, if granted, allows you to work in the United States for up to 12 months. We encourage you to work closely with your career advisor and an advisor in the Office of Global Services.
Is the GMAT or GRE required for admission? 
Northeastern University's D'Amore-McKim School of Business is currently test-optional and does not require applicants to submit GRE or GMAT scores. 
Is a master's degree in Finance worth it? 
If you're considering a Master's in Finance, the decision depends on your career objectives and personal circumstances. The advanced knowledge, specialized skills, and expanded professional network you gain can significantly enhance career prospects, particularly in finance, investment banking, private wealth management, or corporate finance. 
[learn more about the degree and its benefits](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/resources/cc-is-an-ms-in-finance-worth-it/)
Will the program prepare me for the CFA? 
The curriculum is closely aligned with the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation. This alignment provides a strong foundation for those who pursue the designation. Learn more at the [CFA Institute website](https://www.cfainstitute.org/en/). 
What is an MBA internship? 
At D'Amore-McKim, MBA students gain real-world experience through corporate residency—a deeper, longer internship where you work alongside company leaders for 3, 6 or up to 12 months. It's a chance to apply what you've learned, build your network, and make a real impact. 
[learn more about mba internships](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/resources/what-is-an-mba-internship/)
Is the program accredited? 
Yes. The D'Amore-McKim School of Business is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
