#  MinaVidrine
Postdoctoral Teaching Associate, English 
Mina Vidrine joined Northeastern from the University of Oregon, where she taught writing for five years while completing her PhD in English. In 2022, she spent a year teaching writing and literature at Transilvania University of Brașov (Romania) as a Fulbright grantee.
Mina’s research focuses on representations of faith in First World War poetry. She is also interested in the digital humanities and serves as a core TEI editor for the David Jones Digital Archive. She is currently working with the Digital Archive and the Open University (UK) on a digital exhibition of David Jones’s painted inscriptions.
