Program overview 
##  Become a dynamic statistician 
Northeastern University’s Master of Science in Statistics program stands out for its highly curated curriculum, which combines foundational theory with practical, hands-on experiences and opportunities. Designed to meet the soaring demand for statistical knowledge that can be applied across diverse industries, this master’s program not only equips students with essential skills and concepts but also emphasizes real-world applications.
Students within this program can choose from three in-demand and interdisciplinary pathways: biostatistics, statistical machine learning, and statistical theory and modeling. Each offers instruction in advanced statistical skills, experience-based learning opportunities, and valuable connections to industry leaders.

Program objectives 
##  Prepare for success 
Students will build a foundation in advanced statistical skills, then further specialize their knowledge in one of three concentration areas to make impactful, data-driven decisions.
Execute analyses 
Perform appropriate classical statistical, causal, machine learning, and AI analyses based on a solid understanding of the inferential and predictive modeling assumptions and properties.
Develop code 
Create version-controlled and reproducible code using modern statistical software to maintain, analyze, and visualize both small- and massive-scale data.
Explain results 
Communicate and present statistical designs, models, and results effectively across technical and non-technical audiences.
Understand complex experiments 
Using statistical theory, conduct, evaluate, and interpret models and complex experiments to derive and interpret appropriate and/or novel models for data analyses.
