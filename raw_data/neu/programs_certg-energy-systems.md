Program overview 
##  Power up your energy skills 
The Graduate Certificate in Energy Systems Management at Northeastern University equips you with the skills to integrate energy systems engineering with strategic management practices.
This four-course program focuses on analyzing and optimizing energy systems, preparing you to propose effective, data-driven, and economically viable solutions. Ideal for those looking to advance or shift careers, this certificate offers a blend of technical engineering knowledge and management strategies.
The Graduate Certificate in Energy Systems Management at Northeastern University equips you with the skills to integrate energy systems engineering with strategic management practices.
This four-course program focuses on analyzing and optimizing energy systems, preparing you to propose effective, data-driven, and economically viable solutions. Ideal for those looking to advance or shift careers, this certificate offers a blend of technical engineering knowledge and management strategies.
Unique features 
  * Complete four specialized courses focused on energy systems management.
  * Explore energy systems through both technical and management lenses to address real-world challenges.
  * Use this certificate as a pathway to a master’s degree, enhancing career opportunities and potential earnings.


Future outlook 
Graduates with specialized knowledge in energy systems management are positioned to advance in a growing industry. According to the Washington Post, certificate holders can earn a 25% average increase in salary, making this program a valuable investment for career growth and change.
Experiential learning highlight 
Northeastern’s signature experiential learning model prepares students for industry challenges. Gain real-world experience through the cooperative education program (co-op), which places students in professional roles from four to eight months across diverse organizations. Access resources such as resumé building, interview preparation, and professional development to jumpstart your career in energy systems management.
