Program overview 
##  Advance as a learning leader 
The Master of Education in Learning (MEdL) program develops your leadership capabilities with advanced skills in designing, facilitating, and creating curricula for various learning environments. The program prepares you to grow your career in higher education, K–12 settings, and workplace learning.
You’ll engage in a program focused on contemporary education, from understanding learning science to creating inclusive educational experiences and applying research-based approaches. You can personalize your degree to your interests with a choice of four in-demand concentrations.
Throughout the program, you’ll learn to examine education through a social justice perspective and use research and learning science principles to create effective learning experiences.

Accreditation 
The Master of Education in Learning has been approved for initial and add-on licensure by the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education. Students who successfully complete the special education concentration and certificate, or the ESL concentration and certificate, are eligible for endorsement for licensure. For information on where this program will help you achieve professional licensure, please visit Northeastern’s consumer information website and review the professional licensure disclosure. For questions regarding professional licensure, please contact Jennifer Madonna, director of field experience and licensure, by emailing [[email protected]](https://graduate.northeastern.edu/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#076972616e626b636861616e646247696875736f6266747362756929626372).
Concentrations 
Specialize your degree with four industry-aligned concentrations:
  * Learning and instruction:
  * Special education:
  * Learning experience design and technology:
  * English as a second language (ESL)


Unique benefits 
  * Gain career flexibility and the ability to advance in various learning environments.
  * Personalize your degree with one of four distinct concentrations.
  * Practice what you learn through experiential learning opportunities in real-world settings.
  * Engage in a curriculum designed for current K–12 teachers, leaders/managers responsible for professional development in higher education, instructional designers, technologist trainers, community organization leaders, and nonprofit educators.
  * For those pursuing teaching careers in Massachusetts, the program provides pathways to licensure endorsement. Students completing the special education or ESL concentrations qualify for endorsement from the Massachusetts Department of Elementary and Secondary Education.
