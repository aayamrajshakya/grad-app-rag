## Program options
### Build critical skills for leading with impact in international business
Our MS in International Management prepares you to lead across borders with the cultural agility and business expertise to thrive in a global marketplace. In this customizable, experience-powered program, you'll align coursework with your career goals while building the skills to harness data, technology, and strategy to unlock new markets and optimize global operations.

Consider full-time study 
#### Program timeline
As a full-time student, you'll study on our Boston campus as part of a tight-knit class, learning together and sharing experiences as you move through the program. Your courses are thoughtfully planned in a set order over three or four semesters, ending with a project where you'll apply all the skills you've learned as part of a team. With no work experience required for admission, this is a great option for recent graduates.
[Explore Full-time curriculum map](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/DMSB_FullTimeMSIM_Curriculum-Map_FNL.pdf)

Complete required coursework and choose electives 
You'll begin the MS in International Management program by mastering the core concepts that every global leader needs. Then, as early as your second semester, you'll start shaping the degree around your goals.
Here's the best part: half of your credits are electives. That means you have the freedom to dive deep into the areas that matter most to you—whether that's entrepreneurship, finance, marketing, or even courses outside of business in fields like law, healthcare, or computer science.

### Stackable credits
If you're also considering an MBA from D'Amore-McKim, the MS in International Management can get you halfway there. Once your degree is complete—and you've maintained at least a 3.0 GPA and a minimum grade in each class—you may be able to apply your 30 credits toward an eligible MBA program for up to three years following the completion of your degree. Should your application be accepted, you'll be eligible for the [Double Husky scholarship](https://www.northeastern.edu/graduate/double-husky-scholarship/) to reduce your tuition by 25%.

## Applicant FAQs
How do I apply and what will I need? 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our[ application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, answer a few questions, and pay the fee. You'll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)


[Learn more about applying to D'Amore-McKim](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/how-to-apply/)
Are there other application requirements for international students? 
Yes. International students must meet additional requirements, including proof of English language proficiency, a valid passport, and English translations of academic transcripts. Our [international graduate students page](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/admissions/faq/international-graduate-students/) provides more information about applying and the benefits of choosing D'Amore-McKim.
Is the GMAT or GRE required for admission? 
Northeastern University's D'Amore-McKim School of Business is currently test-optional and does not require applicants to submit GRE or GMAT scores.
Is the program accredited? 
Yes. The D'Amore-McKim School of Business is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business International—the highest standard of achievement for business schools worldwide.
