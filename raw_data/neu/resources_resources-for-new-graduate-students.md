Advising and Registration
Search for courses and register via the [Student Hub](https://me.northeastern.edu/) portal. If you are unsure of the courses in which to enroll, review the [Academic Catalog](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/resource/academic-catalog/) and consult the [Graduate Program Administrator](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/cssh-people/?live_wp_people_search%5BrefinementList%5D%5Bstaff_types%5D%5B0%5D=Graduate%20Program%20Administrators) for your program.
Visit the [Registrar’s website](https://service.northeastern.edu/registrar?sys_kb_id=4616f106879d06d0878b0edc0ebb35ae&id=kb_article_view&sysparm_rank=1&sysparm_tsqueryId=6f47396d471fce14a75b2172e36d431b) for detailed class registration instructions.

Awards and Scholarships
For more information about scholarships and awards available to CSSH students, see the [Financial Aid and Awards](https://cssh.northeastern.edu/apply/financial-aid-and-awards/) web page.
If you have an award or scholarship, please note that Student Financial Services posts awards and scholarships to student accounts after the close of registration. In the interim, you should only pay the tuition that you will owe minus your scholarship. If you have questions or concerns please contact gradcssh@northeastern.edu
Billing
You will receive an e-bill shortly after registering for classes. Students with assistantships or scholarships should pay the balance of what is not covered by the award. Visit [the Billing and Payments FAQ ](https://studentfinance.northeastern.edu/billing-payments/billing-faq/) and contact [Student Financial Services](https://northeastern.service-now.com/sfs) with any billing questions.
Direct Deposit 
All students are required to sign up for [direct deposit](https://studentfinance.northeastern.edu/billing-payments/direct-deposit/). Please note that if you are a Stipended Graduate Assistant (SGA: Teaching or Research), your first stipend may be in the form of a paper check. pick up your check, bring your Northeastern student identification card to the Student Employment Office at 175 Richards Hall (Boston) or 232 Mills Hall (Oakland).

Health Report Form
All students enrolled in Massachusetts, Oakland, Global Scholars, London Scholars and NUin must submit proof of immunity to various diseases. The vaccinations required are set by the state and local health departments. Some Bouvé College of Health Sciences students are required to have clinical clearance prior to clinical, internship or practicum.
Please follow the instructions on the University Health and Counseling Services’ (UHCS) [website](https://uhcs.northeastern.edu/forms/university-health-report/). The requirements must be met no later than 30 days prior to the start of classes. Any questions concerning these requirements should be directed to University Health and Counseling Services at:

Parking Permits and Public Transportation
Will you be driving to campus? Student Financial Services in 354 Richards Hall (Boston) issues [parking permits](https://pref.northeastern.edu/alternative-transportation/#_ga=2.59539115.765920620.1618239068-339393658.1594682944) for Northeastern students who wish to commute by car. To obtain a parking decal, students must complete the online parking permit application.
[Discounted MBTA passes](https://huskycard.northeastern.edu/mbta/) are available for students who will be commuting by public transportation.
For Northeastern Oakland students, parking permits and AC Transit passes are issued in 124 Sage Hall (Oakland). Contact parknuoakland@lazparking.com for information.
For Northeastern Seattle students, an Orca card will cover transportation on Seattle’s buses, trains, and ferries. [Learn more about fares and monthly passes](https://orcacard.com/ERG-Seattle/getACard.do?m=2). For fares, [see here](https://kingcounty.gov/en/dept/metro/fares-and-payment/prices). Most students wanting an unlimited monthly pass use the Puget Pass.
Student Email Accounts and Office 365
Faculty, staff, and students have access to Office 365 as members of the Northeastern community. Your Northeastern username followed by @northeastern.edu is your Office 365 username, and it will be used to access all Office 365 services, including Outlook for email. Go to the [Office 365](https://microsoft365.northeastern.edu/) web page for more information, FAQs, and support.

The Office of Global Services (OGS) is an active resource to 13,000 international students and scholars from 147 different nations across the world. OGS provides the professional expertise and support you need to maintain compliance through immigration, academic, and your employment experiences—helping you remain a valuable member of the Northeastern community.
