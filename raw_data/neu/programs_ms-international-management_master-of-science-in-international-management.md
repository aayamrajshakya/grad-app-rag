Program overview 
##  Evolve with the global economy 
Today, organizations, small and large, across all industries continue to experience an increase in global competition and an unprecedented surge in cross-border alliances, mergers, acquisitions, and expansions.
In this interconnected world, skilled international managers access new sources of capital and talent, find new markets, and leverage global supply chains. Our innovative Master of Science in International Management program will prepare you to lead in a continuously evolving global economy by teaching you how to deliver solutions quickly and confidently by leveraging data, technology, and creativity.
A conventional business education won’t prepare you with a global mindset to excel in today’s complex business environment characterized by rapid-fire change. Our D’Amore-McKim MS in International Management program will.

Unique features 
  * Specialized core classes will guide you in developing robust cultural agility, global leadership, and managing multinational enterprises.
  * Individualized coaching is available as early as your first class to help you realize your goals and set you on a personalized path toward success.
  * Pioneering, international faculty are thought leaders in strategic workforce management, cultural agility, and organizational strategy, among others; they are widely known as prominent global scholars and business leaders committed to student success.
  * Receive personalized career guidance from the D’Amore-McKim Graduate Career Center.


Experience-powered learning 
We offer our students experience-powered learning opportunities that bring academic lessons into real-world practice.
  * “Make Your Case” consulting case project-You’ll polish your global acumen as you work with a small team to tackle a real business issue faced by a company aligned with your career aspirations.
  * [Global Field Study](https://damore-mckim.northeastern.edu/programs/global-field-study) is designed to give students intense exposure to the global business environment by immersing them in the business practices and culture of a country abroad.
  * [OPT Work Authorization](https://international.northeastern.edu/ogs/employment/applying-for-post-completion-opt-f-1/)-International students who successfully complete this program are eligible to apply for Optional Practical Training (OPT) work authorization.

Admissions requirements 
To apply, log in—or create an account—on our [application portal](https://enroll.northeastern.edu/apply/), choose your program, and answer a few questions. You’ll need to submit:
  * Application form
  * A personal statement (<=500 words)
  * Resumé
  * Two letters of recommendation
  * Academic transcript(s) from all undergraduate and graduate schools attended
  * Kira online interview (by invitation, access provided after submitting your application)
  * Optional GMAT/GRE test scores
  * TOEFL, IELTS, Duolingo, or PTE Academic (if applicable)
  * Passport or permanent residency card (if applicable)
