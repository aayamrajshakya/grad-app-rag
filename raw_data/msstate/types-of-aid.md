##  Financial Aid Overview   

Student financial aid is financial assistance to help cover the cost of your college education. It includes scholarships, grants, loans, work-study programs. Financial aid is meant to supplement not replace the financial resources you and your family can provide toward your education.

##  How to Apply for Federal Financial Aid   

  1. Submit the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA)
  2. Submit any additional required documents.
  3. Check your financial aid award package.
  4. Accept or decline your aid in [myState](https://mybanner.msstate.edu/BannerExtensibility/customPage/page/msuFAAcceptDeclineAwards).
  5. Maintain eligibility by meeting good SAP and enrollment requirements.


FAFSA Deadlines:
  * Final Deadline 2024-2025: June 30, 2025
  * Final Deadline 2025-2026: June 30, 2026

##  General Eligibility Requirements   

  * Be admitted into a degree program, [Exceptions to this Policy](https://www.sfa.msstate.edu/types-of-aid/general-student-eligibility-requirements/non-degree-students).
  * Be enrolled in eligible coursework.
  * Be enrolled in the correct number of credit hours. [Learn More About Enrollment and Financial Aid Eligibility](https://www.sfa.msstate.edu/enrollment-and-financial-aid-eligibility).
  * Be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen.
  * Maintain [Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)](https://www.sfa.msstate.edu/satisfactory-academic-progress).
  * Not be in default on a federal student loan.
  * Not borrowed in excess of annual or aggregate limits of federal student loan.
  * Not owe a refund or repayment on a federal grant.
  * Use aid funds only for educational purposes.
  * Meet other requirements as specified by federal, state, and institutional regulations and policies.
  * Incarcerated students have limited eligibility but may be eligible for federal student aid, [Learn More](https://studentaid.gov/understand-aid/eligibility/requirements/criminal-convictions).
