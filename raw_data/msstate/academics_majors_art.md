##  Overview 
The Mississippi State University Department of Art offers the Bachelor of Fine Arts degree. [Accredited by the National Association of Schools of Art and Design (NASAD)](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/our-difference/accreditation/art), the department offers three concentrations: fine arts (including emphasis areas in ceramics, drawing, painting, printmaking, and sculpture), graphic design, and [photography](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/videos/msu-department-art-photography-concentration).
[Learn How To Apply](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/academics/majors/art#admissions) [Watch Our Video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BD7shKYHygQ&t=6s "Mississippi State Department of Art video") [Request Info](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/contact/request-info)

##  Curriculum 
### Hands-on, studio learning: 
The Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) degree is a professional studio degree. The Bachelor of Fine Arts degree is earned after successful completion of an intensive, four-year program that provides the student with a series of in-depth studio experiences leading to a thesis/senior presentation balanced by studies in humanities, communication, mathematics, and sciences.
[Minors](https://catalog.msstate.edu/undergraduate/collegesanddegreeprograms/collegeofarchitectureartanddesign/departmentofart/#minortext) in photography, graphic design, art history and fine art are open to all students at MSU. 
All art majors – “Art-Foundations (ARTF)” – are required to participate in the Foundation Portfolio Review.
[Foundation Portfolio Review Requirements](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/academics/majors/art/portfolio-review)
[Full curriculum requirements for graduation](http://catalog.msstate.edu/undergraduate/collegesanddegreeprograms/collegeofarchitectureartanddesign/departmentofart/#programsofstudytext%20) [Full list of courses available](http://catalog.msstate.edu/undergraduate/collegesanddegreeprograms/collegeofarchitectureartanddesign/departmentofart/#coursestext%20)

##  Admissions  
Pursue a Bachelor of Fine Arts Degree 
Students wishing to pursue the Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) degree at Mississippi State University will be assigned a major of “Art-Undeclared (UART).” Students will declare their concentration (fine arts, graphic design, or photography) following successful passage of the Foundation Portfolio Review (see above)

##  Student Achievement  
Scholarships 
In its commitment to recognize outstanding students whose academic credentials confirm their potential for success, Mississippi State University offers scholarships to students with outstanding ACT scores and to National Merit and National Achievement semifinalists and finalists. For more information on MSU scholarships please contact the [Office of Admissions and Scholarships](http://www.admissions.msstate.edu/scholarships/).
* * *
Each year, the Mississippi State University Department of Art also recognizes student achievement through scholarships and annual awards. These monetary awards are made possible by friends and alumni of the school and are awarded on the basis of financial need, academic standing, and design excellence. 
[Art-Specific Scholarships](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/scholarships/art)
##  Affordable  
Tuition and Fees 
Mississippi State University’s affordable tuition provides an excellent return on your investment, combining competitive costs with the best academics, research, student life, and career preparation.
[Tuition at Mississippi State University](https://www.admissions.msstate.edu/freshmen/money-matters/#tuition)
The Bachelor of Fine Arts undergraduate degree program has some additional fees required for materials, technology, and laboratory materials that enhance an art student’s education that are automatically assessed to students. The Mississippi State University Department of Art strives to be able to offer comparatively low lab fees thanks to support from alumni and friends.
Students also need to purchase a variety of tools and materials to help them succeed in their studio coursework.
* * *
### Required Equipment, Tools, and Materials 
The Department of Art requires all incoming Art majors to purchase certain technology and equipment necessary for production and presentation of artwork within departmental courses.
[Full Equipment, Tools, and Materials Requirements](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/current-students/art/materials-fees)

##  Student Life  
Studio learning is a unique experience 
Mississippi State University Department of Art provides a supportive environment for personal growth and professional development. 
One big advantage of studio learning is it allows for more students to have more one-on-one time with professors; the Mississippi State University Bachelor of Art degree program boasts a low 15:1 student to faculty ratio.
[Life as a Student](https://www.caad.msstate.edu/academics/majors/art/student-life)

##  Our  
Mission 
The Department of Art's primary undergraduate responsibilities include educating professional artists with emphases in ceramics, drawing, graphic design, painting, photography, printmaking, and sculpture; preparing students for a career or advanced study; offering courses that fulfill university requirements; and providing an active art gallery to serve the University, the community, and region.
