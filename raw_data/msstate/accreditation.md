# Accreditation
Mississippi State University is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award baccalaureate, master's, specialist, and doctoral degrees. MSU has been accredited by SACSCOC since 1926 with its most recent reaffirmation in 2024. More information is available at <https://www.sacscoc.org/>.
The College of Education at Mississippi State University has enjoyed long-standing accreditation on national, regional, and state levels. On a national level, the Council for the Accreditation of Educator Preparation
Additionally, CAEP awarded us the [Frank Murray Leadership Recognition for Continuous Improvement](https://www.msstate.edu/newsroom/article/2024/05/msus-college-education-receives-national-frank-murray-leadership), a reflection of our commitment to strengthening P-12 learning.

At the state level, our educator preparation programs are approved by the [Mississippi Department of Education](https://www.mdek12.org/ "https://www.mdek12.org/").
Some of the departments in the College of Education have program areas that maintain discipline-specific accreditation and approval.
The Department of Counseling, Higher Education Leadership, Educational Psychology, and Foundationsâ€™ School Psychology program is accredited by the American Psychological Association (APA) and approved by the National Associations of School Psychologists (NASP). The Clinical Mental Health, School, and Rehabilitation Counseling programs are accredited by the Council for Accreditation of Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP). These accreditations are necessary for professional licensure for each discipline. 
The programs in the Department of Music are accredited by the National Association of Schools of Music ([NASM](http://nasm.arts-accredit.org/)).
