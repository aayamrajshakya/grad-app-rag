# Business Technologies
The Business Technologies group at the Tepper School is home to quantitative researchers who study how technology affects business and society from an economics lens. The group is interested in design, measurement, and policy issues that result from advances in technologies such as:  

  * Machine learning/artificial intelligence
  * Blockchain/cryptocurrencies
  * Automated machines
  * Augmented reality
  * Future of work
  * Robo-advisors


  
As a [top-ranked group in the world for Information Systems discipline](https://www.usnews.com/best-graduate-schools/top-business-schools/information-systems-rankings), no other business school has produced more [Informs Information System Society Distinguished Fellows](https://www.cmu.edu/tepper/faculty-and-research/academic-areas/business-technologies#paragraph-1358).

### Informs ISS Distinguished Fellow Award Winners
The most prestigious award for the Information Systems discipline, the [Informs ISS Distinguished Fellow Awards](https://www.informs.org/Recognizing-Excellence/Community-Prizes/Information-Systems-Society/ISS-Distinguished-Fellow-Award) recognizes individuals who have made lifelong outstanding contributions to the Information Systems discipline in research, education, and service.
The Tepper Business Technologies group has an exceptional record in producing Informs ISS Distinguished Fellows, including:
Andrew Whinston (Inaugural Class 2009)
Charles Kriebel (Inaugural Class 2009)
Prabuddha De (Inaugural Class 2009)
Tridas Mukhopadhyay (2012)
Chris Kemerer (2013)
Sandra Slaughter (2014)
Anitesh Barua (2015)
Anindya Ghose (2015)
Chris Forman (2019)
Param Vir Singh (2019)
Michael D. Smith (2024)
