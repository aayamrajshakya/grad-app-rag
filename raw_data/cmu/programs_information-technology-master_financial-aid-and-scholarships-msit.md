# Affording Your Online Master's Degree in Information Technology
* * *
Your education is one of the most valuable investments you will ever make. We can help you pay for it.
Below, you will find detailed information on scholarships, student employment opportunities, and loans to help offset the cost of your online Master of Science in Information Technology education. If you have any questions about student finances, please contact the Office of Admissions via email at hnzadmit@andrew.cmu.edu, or give us a call at 412-268-2164.
## Scholarships
* * *
Applicants to the program can be considered for any of the following applicable scholarship opportunities:
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "Merit-Based Scholarships \(application may be required\)")
###### Master of Science in information technology Program Scholarships
These merit-based scholarships are awarded to exceptional students joining the Master of Science in Information Technology program on the basis of academic and/or professional achievement.
###### Pittsburgh Regional Leaders Scholarships
Heinz College offers a limited number of Regional Leaders Scholarships to incoming full- and part-time students who have a demonstrated a commitment to the Pittsburgh community. If you are interested in a Regional Leaders Scholarship, we require that an individual who can testify to your commitment to and impact on the Pittsburgh community writes at least one of your recommendations for admission.
###### Excellence in Technology Fellowships
Heinz College offers a limited number of half-tuition Excellence in Technology Fellowships each year. These fellowships are awarded to exceptional students joining the Master of Science in Information Technology program. Awards will be based on the student’s potential for excellence and leadership in the field of information technology and management. 
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "Employment-Based Scholarships")
###### City of Pittsburgh/Allegheny County Scholarship Program
Heinz College awards enrolling City of Pittsburgh and Allegheny County employees a minimum tuition scholarship of 35 percent per semester (may be awarded as an MSIT Program Scholarship).*
###### Nonprofit, Municipal and Government Employee Scholarships
Heinz College awards enrolling Municipal and Government (U.S. federal, state, or local level) and nonprofit organizations' employees a minimum tuition scholarship of 25 percent per semester (may be awarded as a Master of Science in Information Technology Program Scholarship).*
###### Pittsburgh Water and Sewer Authority (PWSA)
Heinz College awards Pittsburgh Water and Sewer Authority (PWSA) employees a minimum tuition scholarship of 30 percent per semester (may be awarded as a Master of Science in Information Technology Program Scholarship).*
*In order to qualify for the "Employment-Based" scholarships, you must provide written verification of employment on your employer's letterhead, signed by your supervisor, with the application for admission. You will be asked to submit verification of continued eligible employment to the Master of Science in Information Technology program office annually.
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "Partnership-Based Scholarships")
###### Heinz College Strategic Partners Scholarships
Heinz College has partnered with a number of organizations to provide financial support to their members in order to reduce the cost of attending graduate school. If you are affiliated with one or more of these partner organizations, you are eligible for a Strategic Partners Scholarship from Heinz College as a result of that affiliation. In order to be considered, you should indicate on your application for admission your experience or involvement with one or more of the partner organizations. 
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "Regional and National Education Partners Scholarships")
Incoming master's students who have completed their bachelor's degree at a regional or national education partner of Heinz College are awarded a Regional or National Education Partner scholarship. Heinz College awards a scholarship of 30% up to 100% of tuition to eligible students enrolling on a full-time basis. [Find a full of the partner schools.](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/admissions/strategic-partner-scholarship-program.php)
###### Heinz College Executive Education Alumni Scholarships
If you are a student who has completed one of Heinz College’s Executive Education certificate programs, you are eligible for a tuition discount, reducing the cost of tuition for completing your Master of Science in Information Technology degree.
Certificate programs within Heinz College Executive Education include the Chief Information Officer (CIO), Chief Information Security Officer (CISO), Chief Risk Officer (CRO), Chief Data Officer (CDataO), Chief Digital Officer (CDigitalO), and Public Interest Technology Certificate (PITC) programs. Any tuition that either you or your sponsor/employer has paid for completing these certificate programs (up to $40,000) will be matched with a tuition discount from the MSIT program, thereby reducing the cost of earning your Master of Science in Information Technology degree by up to $40,000.
To be considered for the Executive Education Alumni Scholarship, please indicate your enrollment status with the CIO, CISO, CRO, CDataO, CDigitalO and/or PITC Program(s) on your application for admission to the MSIT program.
Please note: The Executive Education alumni tuition discount is only available once a student has completed all of the certificate program’s requirements.
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "United States Military Veterans Scholarships/Benefits")
###### U.S. Military Veterans Scholarship
U.S. military veterans who are not receiving 100 percent tuition benefits through the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs are eligible to receive a scholarship of up to 30 percent of tuition costs. This scholarship benefit is available to the spouses and children of U.S. military veterans. Applicants should indicate their eligibility within their application for admission.
Students can be awarded additional scholarship support above and beyond this particular award by the Admissions Committee.
###### Other U.S. Military Veterans Benefits
U.S. Military veterans can learn more about available benefits on our [Veterans Education Benefits page](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/admissions/checklist/veterans.php).
###### Fallen Heroes Scholarship Program
The Heinz College Fallen Heroes Scholarship program aims to provide scholarship support to the surviving spouses/partners and children of our fallen heroes. See our [Fallen Heroes Scholarship Program page](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/admissions/checklist/fallen-heroes-program.php) for additional information on program eligibility and benefits.
  * [ ](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/programs/information-technology-master/financial-aid-and-scholarships-msit# "General Heinz College Scholarship Information")
If you are eligible for more than one scholarship from Heinz College, you may not be entitled to the minimum award for the second scholarship. Typically, scholarships or scholarship percentages offered by Heinz College are renewable for subsequent semesters of enrollment. However, any scholarship or fellowship offered may be reduced or rescinded for any of the following reasons:
    * Changing the academic program of study
    * A difference in the number of enrolled units originally used to calculate eligibility
    * Extending the normal program length
    * Deferment of offer of admission
    * Failure to make satisfactory academic progress
    * Financial aid overaward*
*Total scholarship from all sources generally may not exceed the cost of tuition and fees. Total financial aid from all sources may not exceed the total cost of attendance.
See [CMU's Statement of Assurance](https://www.cmu.edu/policies/administrative-and-governance/statement-of-assurance.html).


EXTERNAL SCHOLARSHIPS
“External" or “outside” scholarships are any scholarships not awarded by the university.
Generally speaking, searching for outside scholarships can be a tedious process if you don't know where to start. [Visit Carnegie Mellon's website](https://www.cmu.edu/sfs/financial-aid/types/private/outside-scholarships.html) to help you get started on your search for external scholarship support from a broad range of external organizations and sources. 
Additional scholarship suggestions are available in the [FAQs section](https://www.heinz.cmu.edu/admissions/faq/financial-aid) of our website.
Students in the Information Security and Assurance program are eligible for a number of other external scholarships specific to the field of information security and cybersecurity. [Learn more about these awards](https://cmu.app.box.com/s/4tpx1jrzhvtdza3ncr0dhmclnau7rweg). Those students can also utilize the[Cybersecurity Scholarship Guide](https://cybersecurityguide.org/resources/scholarship-guide/ "https://cybersecurityguide.org/resources/scholarship-guide/") to focus their search for field-related awards they can use to support their graduate education.
Resources specific to the Information Technology Management and Business Intelligence and Data Analytics tracks are forthcoming. 
##### Loans
As a Heinz College student, you may be eligible for federal direct loans or private education loans. For more information on the loans for which you may be eligible, including detailed instructions for filling out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) form, [please visit the Carnegie Mellon University Student Financial Services website](http://www.cmu.edu/sfs/financial-aid/types/index.html).
##### Costs
Please visit the student Financial Services website for the [estimated cost of attendance](https://www.cmu.edu/sfs/tuition/graduate/heinz/mpm-msit-2526.html) for Master of Science in Information Technology students before scholarships. All figures are estimates and are subject to change. These budgets provide you with information about costs associated with attending the program and serve as the basis for determining federal financial aid awards.
