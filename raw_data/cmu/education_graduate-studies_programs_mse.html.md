## Master’s degree programs
  * [Master of Science in Materials Science (Research)](https://www.mse.engineering.cmu.edu/education/graduate/masters-programs/ms-materials.html)  
The Master of Science in Materials Science degree combines graduate-level coursework and an individual research project to provide an advanced foundational education to professionals interested in advancing their knowledge in the field. Graduates of the program have a fundamental understanding of materials structure, properties, and the processing of materials and their applications in various areas of engineering.
  * [Master of Science in Materials Science and Engineering (Coursework)](https://www.mse.engineering.cmu.edu/education/graduate/masters-programs/materials-engineering.html)  
The Master of Science in Materials Science and Engineering is a full-time coursework-based degree that provides an advanced foundational education to professionals interested in advancing their knowledge in Materials Science and Engineering. Graduates of the program gain a fundamental understanding of the structure, properties, and processing of materials and their applications in various areas of engineering.
  * [Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence Engineering - Materials Science and Engineering (MS in AIE - MSE)](https://www.mse.engineering.cmu.edu/education/graduate/masters-programs/ms-ai-engineering.html)  
Students in the Masters of Science in Artificial Intelligence Engineering - Material Science program learn how to design and build AI-orchestrated systems capable of operating within engineering constraints. Carnegie Mellon is leading this transformation by teaching students how to simultaneously design a system’s functionality and support AI mechanisms to produce more adaptable, resilient, and trustworthy systems.
  * **[Master of Science in Energy Science, Technology, and Policy](https://www.cmu.edu/engineering/estp/)**  
The Energy Science, Technology and Policy program offers distinctive and customizable professional Master of Science degrees in energy. Each of the four energy master's degrees are based in engineering, aligned with new discoveries in science, attuned to sustainability and the environment, and informed by a broader perspective in economics and public policy.
  * **[Dual Master of Science in Materials Science and Engineering and Technology Innovation Management](https://www.mse.engineering.cmu.edu/education/graduate/masters-programs/dual-degrees.html)  
** The Master of Science in Materials Science and Engineering and Technology Innovation Management (E&TIM) program offers students with technical backgrounds exposure to technology innovation and management related to engineering practice.**
