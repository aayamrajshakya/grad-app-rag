## Bio
Thomas Costello is an Assistant Professor in Social and Decision Sciences at Carnegie Mellon University. He also holds appointments as Affiliated Faculty at the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University and is a Research Affiliate at MIT’s Sloan School of Management. His research integrates psychology, political science, and human-computer interaction to examine where our viewpoints come from, how they differ from person to person, and why they change. He also studies the sweeping impacts of artificial intelligence on these belief- and society-related processes.
Dr. Costello’s work has been supported by federal agencies, philanthropic foundations, and corporate sponsors, and has been published in top peer-reviewed outlets, including Science, Nature Nature Medicine Journal of Personality and Social Psychology Psychological BulletinTrends in Cognitive SciencesThe New York TimesBBC World NewsNPR[DebunkBot.com](https://DebunkBot.com), a public tool for combating conspiracy theories with AI. He has been awarded the Rising Star award from the Association for Psychological Science, the Klarman Fellowship from Cornell University, the Heritage Dissertation Research Award from the Society for Personality and Social Psychology, and the JS Tanaka Dissertation Award from the Association for Research in Personality.
