# Applying for Tuition Benefits
Faculty and staff members applying for tuition benefits for the first time should review the following information and the relevant [tuition benefit-related page(s)](https://www.cmu.edu/hr/benefits/tuition/index.html). If you have questions regarding tuition benefits, contact HR Services.
## Employee Taking Courses at CMU
### Step 1: Discuss using tuition benefits with your supervisor
  * Meet with your supervisor to discuss the program or course you would like to pursue. Discuss how enrollment may affect your workload and schedule.
  * Complete the Supervisor Approval Form [pdf] including your signature as well as your supervisor's signature.
    * Please note: It is the responsibility of the employee's supervisor to review the form in its entirety and certify whether the employee's graduate-level courses (or degree program of which they are a part) are CMU job-related per the IRS job-related criteria detailed in the form[taxation of tuition benefits](https://www.cmu.edu/hr/benefits/tuition/taxation.html).


### Step 2: Finalize your course schedule
  * If you have applied and been accepted to a formal degree program,[Student Information Online](https://s3.as.cmu.edu/sio/index.html).
  * If you aren't in a formal degree program,[Non-Degree Student](https://www.cmu.edu/hub/registrar/registration/vnd/faculty-staff.html). Note: Non-Degree Students must complete two separate applications if enrolling in a graduate and undergraduate course in the same term.


### Step 3: Apply through Workday
  * Refer to the Applying for Employee Tuition Benefits Quick Guide [pdf] for detailed instructions.
    * A new application for tuition benefits must be completed for each term.
    * Your application will not be reviewed until you are registered for courses.
  * You will receive an email confirming the submission of your tuition application for processing and a second email once your application has been approved for payment.
  * After receiving approval, a tuition remission transaction will be posted to your student account.


## Employee Taking Courses at Another Institution
### Step 1: Discuss using tuition benefits with your supervisor
  * Meet with your supervisor to discuss the program or course you would like to pursue. Discuss how enrollment may affect your workload and schedule.
  * Complete the Supervisor Approval Form [pdf] including your signature as well as your supervisor's signature.Please note: 
    * The courses taken at an accredited institution other than CMU, or degree program of which they are a part, are CMU career-related
    * Whether or not the employee's graduate-level courses (or degree program of which they are a part) are CMU job-related per the IRS job-related criteria detailed in the form[taxation of tuition benefits](https://www.cmu.edu/hr/benefits/tuition/taxation.html).


### Step 2: Finalize your course schedule
  * Register for course(s)


### Step 3: Apply through Workday
  * Refer to the Applying for Employee Tuition Benefits Quick Guide [pdf] for instructions.
    * You will be required to submit a copy of the course description(s) with the number of credits per class and an invoice showing tuition charges.
    * A new application for tuition benefits must be completed for each term.
    * Your application will not be reviewed until you are registered for courses.
  * You will receive an email confirming the submission of your tuition application for processing and a second email once your application has been approved for payment.
  * CMU will remit payment of your tuition assistance directly to the institution,
    * If you have already paid tuition charges, ask the other institution for a refund in the amount of CMU's payment.


## Dependent Child Taking Courses at CMU or Another Institution
  1. Your child should register for the course(s)
  2. Apply for Tuition Benefits for Dependent Children[Workday](https://wd5.myworkday.com/cmu/d/home.htmld) as early as possible. 
     * Refer to the [Applying for Dependent Tuition Benefits Quick Guide [pdf]](https://www.cmu.edu/my-workday-toolkit/quick-guides/restricted/quick-guide-dependent-tuition-benefits.pdf) for instructions.
     * If your child is attending another institution, upload a copy of the invoice showing tuition charges.
     * A new application is required for EACH semester in which you are requesting benefits for your child.
  3. The tuition benefit will be paid as follows:
     * If your child is attending CMU
     * If your child is attending another institution


## Course Schedule Changes After Tuition Benefit Is Applied
If your course schedule changes after a tuition benefit has been submitted, please notify HR Services immediately at 412-268-4600 or hr-help@andrew.cmu.edu. There may need to be adjustments to the benefit amount and/or taxation.
# Understanding Taxation
The tax implications of tuition benefits can vary depending on several factors, including the level of study, the purpose of the courses and your state of residence. Learn more about [taxation and tuition benefits](https://www.cmu.edu/hr/benefits/tuition/taxation.html).
# Loss of Eligibility
If you (or your dependent) lose eligibility for the benefithr-help@andrew.cmu.edu; benefits will continue only through the completion of the current semester.
