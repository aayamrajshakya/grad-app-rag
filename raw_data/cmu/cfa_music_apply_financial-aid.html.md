# Financial Aid
Below you will find some information about general financial aid. We encourage you to visit Carnegie Mellon's [financial aid page](https://www.cmu.edu/sfs/financial-aid/index.html) for more specific information.
## Undergraduate Aid
Carnegie Mellon's financial aid program is purely need-based, with some limited merit aid - provided by the School of Music directly - potentially being available for Music students. Applicants don't need to submit any additional materials or requirements other than what is specified below.
  * If you’re a US citizen or permanent resident, the FAFSA is required to be considered for federal and state financial aid programs.
  * The CSS PROFILE is required to be considered for all institutional  
  
Prospective students are encouraged to reach out to the Office of[Undergraduate Admission ](https://www.cmu.edu/admission/costs-aid)with any questions about financial aid. Email at admission@andrew.cmu.edu or call (412)268-2082. 


## Graduate Aid
The School of Music at Carnegie Mellon University offers competitive fellowships and assistantships based on merit and institutional need. 
## Eligibility* & Requirements
  * ADVANCED MUSIC STUDIES (AMS) - fellowshipAMS candidates are not eligible for assistantships.
  * MASTER'S (MM, MS) - fellowship and assistantship[Acceptd application](https://app.getacceptd.com/cmumusic).
    * Graduate Assistantships include a stipend of $10,000 per yearwork up to 10 hours per week for their assigned supervisor.

_*Fellowships and Assistantships are not available for Music Education Certificate candidates._
