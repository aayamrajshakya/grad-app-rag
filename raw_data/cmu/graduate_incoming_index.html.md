# Incoming Graduate Students and Postdocs
Welcome to Carnegie Mellon University! As a new graduate student or postdoc, you may have a broad range of questions. The Office of Graduate and Postdoctoral Affairs is here to support you as you transition to life as a graduate student or postdoc. 
Please access the appropriate section below to review some of the actions items you should take upon beginning your CMU journey as a graduate student or postdoc.

#  Incoming Graduate Student Action Items 
Action Item |  Spring Deadline |  Fall Deadline  
---|---|---  
Submit your application for a [campus parking permit](https://www.cmu.edu/transportation/park/permits.html), if needed.  |  Immediately |  Immediately  
Review the [Tech Quick Start Guide](https://www.cmu.edu/computing/start/students.html)[ for Students](https://www.cmu.edu/computing/start/students.html) to prepare your tech for life on campus |  Immediately |  Immediately  
Complete your [student employee verification](https://www.cmu.edu/student-affairs/theword/community-policies/employment-verification.html), if applicable. |  January |  August  
Attend the [University-wide New Graduate Student Orientation](https://www.cmu.edu/graduate/news-and-events/orientation/index.html) _._ Note: International students are required to attend an additional orientation hosted by the [Office of International Education (OIE)](https://www.cmu.edu/oie/community-connections/orientation/index.html). Contact OIE for details. |  January |  August  
Complete the Doctoral Student Orientation or Master's Student Orientation |  January |  August  
In addition to these action items, graduate students are encouraged to complete the [New Graduate Student Onboarding Checklist](https://www.cmu.edu/hub/checklist/new-grad-onboarding.pdf), which covers required and recommended tasks to complete in the summer before you start your CMU experience.   
  
International students should visit the [Office of International Education (OIE) site](https://www.cmu.edu/oie/community-connections/orientation/index.html) for important steps to receive the proper immigration documents required to travel to the U.S. and maintain legal status once you're in the U.S.
