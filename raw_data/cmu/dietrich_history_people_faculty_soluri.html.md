## Bio
Professor Soluri's research and teaching explore the relationship between social and environmental change in Latin America. His book,Creatures of Fashion: Animals, Global Markets, the Transformation of Patagonia
New research projects includeCoffee Break.
He is also working on research related to histories of petroleum extraction in Patagonia and agricultural diversity and cuisine in Latin America.His earlier work on export banana production and consumption is available in a second edition (2021) of his award-winning book,Banana Cultures[TedEd education ](https://www.ted.com/talks/john_soluri_the_dark_history_of_bananas?language=en)[video](https://www.ted.com/talks/john_soluri_the_dark_history_of_bananas?language=en).
He is currently working with two graduate students:[Matthew Turetsky](https://www.cmu.edu/dietrich/history/people/graduate-students/turetsky.html)[Zach Crouch](https://www.cmu.edu/dietrich/history/people/graduate-students/zach-crouch.html) who is interested in the Mexican Revolution's transnational influences on struggles for land in the Americas.
