# Experiential Learning
MSCF students are offered many ways to engage with the industry, alumni, peers, and other academic institutions via initiatives that allow them to apply skills being taught in the classroom.
## MSCF Speaker Series[](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/academics/speaker-series.html)
## [](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/academics/speaker-series.html)
Every week in the fall semester, industry professionals deliver [focused talks](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/academics/speaker-series.html).
## Question of the Day
Students compete for the top spot on the leaderboard by correctly answering our daily MarketWatch questions.
## Curatia News
All students in the MSCF Program receive a daily feed of curated news featuring the latest intelligence on unfolding industry trends, markets, tech innovations, regulatory developments, and the occasional industry gossip.
## MSCF Trading Competition
[](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/competitions/trading-competition.html)
## Rotman Trading Competition
MSCF sponsors a team of students who [compete against ](https://inside.rotman.utoronto.ca/financelab/competition/ritc/)[50+ universities](https://inside.rotman.utoronto.ca/financelab/competition/ritc/) by identifying trading opportunities across a wide range of realistic simulated scenarios.
## Datathon
[](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/images/assets/datathon_2024.png)
MSCF hosts teams of CMU students who build a [winning trading strategy](https://www.cmu.edu/mscf/news/2024/2024-datathon-competition.html) with the assistance of mentors from Quantbot Technologies, LP.
## Quantitative Problem Solving
Students are presented with challenging sets of questions over a course of six seminars to help prepare for interviews.
## Market Making Game
[](https://www.cmu.edu/mcs/news-events/2024/0408_market-making-game.html)
Students from across Carnegie Mellon University emulate Wall Street traders during the [Quant Club's](https://cmuquant.com/) annual Market Making Game.
