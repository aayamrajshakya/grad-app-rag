#  Deirdre Clemente, Ph.D., 2010 
May 14, 2018
Deirdre Clemente is a scholar of the American clothing industry and studies dress as social and cultural change. She is the author of Dress Casual: How College Kids Redefined American Style [East Coast/West Coast: A History of the American Fashion Industry](https://deirdre-clemente-475a.squarespace.com/eastcoastwestcoast/) plots the development of New York and Los Angeles as global fashion capitals. Sources include: designer’s production notes; radio scripts for textile promotion in the 1940s; women’s diaries; and the Chamber of Commerce papers for the city of Beverly Hills. Clemente’s work aims tohelp us better understand how clothing shapes the human experience.
Deirdre Clemente is the director of [public history](https://deirdre-clemente-475a.squarespace.com/who-am-i/) and an associate professor of history at UNLV. She has published in journals including the Journal of Social HistoryThe New England QuarterlyJournal of American Ethnic History _[The New York Times](https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/28/world/europe/france-burkini-bikini-ban.html)_ , USATodayThe Washington PostThe Wall Street JournalGQ[The Atlantic](https://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2017/05/history-of-business-casual/526014/), Harper’s Bazaar
Dr. Clemente has a degree in Writing Seminars from The Johns Hopkins University and an MA in Museum Studies: Costumes and Textiles from the Fashion Institute of Technology (FIT).
